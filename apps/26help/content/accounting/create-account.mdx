## What this does

Adds a new account to your chart of accounts. You choose the account code, name, and type. The code you pick determines which category the account falls into and where it appears on your reports.

## When to use it

Create a custom account when the default chart does not have a category specific enough for your needs. For example, you might want separate expense accounts for different types of marketing spend.

## Step by step

1. Go to **Chart of Accounts** from the main navigation
2. Click **Create Account**
3. Enter an **Account Code** — pick a number within the correct range for the type you want:
   - **1000-1999** for an asset account
   - **2000-2999** for a liability account
   - **3000-3999** for an equity account
   - **4000-4999** for an income account
   - **5000-6999** for a cost of goods sold account
   - **7000-9998** for an expense account
4. Enter a clear **Account Name** (e.g. "Google Ads" or "Subcontractor Labour")
5. Select the **Account Type** from the dropdown — this must match the code range you chose
6. Add an optional description to help your team understand what this account is for
7. Click **Save**
8. The new account will appear in your chart and be available when categorising transactions, creating journals, and running reports
