Switching accounting software is one of those tasks that businesses put off because it feels risky. What if data gets lost? What if the new platform does not handle things the same way? What if it takes weeks to set up?

The reality is that migrating from Xero to Relentify is a structured process that most small businesses complete in under a day. With proper preparation, you can switch without disrupting your operations or losing any financial data.

This guide walks through every step, from pre-migration preparation to post-migration verification.

## When to migrate

Timing matters. The best time to switch accounting software is at the start of a new financial period — ideally the beginning of your financial year, or at minimum the start of a new VAT quarter.

Migrating at a period boundary means you can close your books in Xero for the completed period, then start fresh in Relentify with a clean opening balance. This approach is simpler than migrating mid-period, where you would need to transfer in-progress transactions.

If you cannot wait for a period boundary, mid-period migration is still possible — it just requires more careful handling of the transition.

## Pre-migration checklist

Before exporting anything from Xero, complete these steps:

**1. Reconcile all bank accounts in Xero.** Ensure every bank feed transaction is matched, categorised, or explained. You do not want unreconciled items creating discrepancies between your Xero data and your Relentify opening balances.

**2. Process outstanding invoices and bills.** Send any draft invoices, record any received payments, and enter any outstanding bills. The cleaner your data in Xero, the easier the migration.

**3. Complete and submit any pending VAT returns.** If you have an outstanding VAT return period, complete and submit it through Xero before migrating. You do not want to split a VAT period across two platforms.

**4. Run key reports in Xero and save them.** Generate and save PDF copies of your profit and loss, balance sheet, trial balance, and aged receivables and payables reports. These serve as your verification documents after migration.

**5. Note your chart of accounts structure.** Review your chart of accounts in Xero and note any custom accounts you have created. Relentify's default chart of accounts covers standard UK accounting, but you may need to add custom accounts to match your Xero setup.

## Step 1: Export your data from Xero

Xero allows you to export data in CSV format. Export the following:

**Chart of accounts:** Settings > Chart of Accounts > Export. This gives you a CSV of all your account codes, names, and types.

**Customer list:** Contacts > Customers > Export. This includes names, email addresses, and contact details.

**Supplier list:** Contacts > Suppliers > Export. Same format as customers.

**Trial balance:** Reports > Trial Balance. Run this for the date you are migrating (typically your financial year end or period end). Export as CSV.

**Outstanding invoices:** Reports > Aged Receivables Detail. This shows all unpaid invoices with amounts and due dates.

**Outstanding bills:** Reports > Aged Payables Detail. This shows all unpaid bills.

**Fixed asset register:** If you have fixed assets recorded in Xero, export the register from Accounting > Fixed Assets.

## Step 2: Set up your Relentify account

Create your Relentify account and configure the basics:

**Company details:** Enter your company name, registration number, VAT number, and registered address.

**Financial year:** Set your financial year start date to match your Xero setup.

**VAT scheme:** Select your VAT scheme (standard, flat rate, or cash accounting) to match your current setup.

**Bank accounts:** Add your bank accounts. If you use Open Banking, you can connect them directly for automatic bank feeds.

## Step 3: Import your chart of accounts

Relentify provides a standard UK chart of accounts by default. You have two options:

**Option A: Use Relentify's default chart of accounts.** If your Xero chart was relatively standard, the default Relentify chart will cover most of your needs. You can add any custom accounts manually.

**Option B: Import your Xero chart of accounts.** Upload the CSV you exported from Xero. Relentify will match account types automatically and flag any that need manual mapping.

For most small businesses, starting with the default chart and adding a handful of custom accounts is faster than importing the full Xero chart.

## Step 4: Import customers and suppliers

Upload your customer and supplier CSV files. Relentify maps the standard fields automatically: name, email, address, phone number, and VAT number.

Review the import preview to ensure data has mapped correctly before confirming.

## Step 5: Enter opening balances

This is the most important step. Your opening balances establish the starting point for your Relentify accounts and must match the closing balances from Xero exactly.

Use the trial balance you exported from Xero as your source. In Relentify, navigate to Settings > Opening Balances and enter the balance for each account.

Alternatively, use Relentify's opening balance import template to upload the balances in bulk. The template matches the format of Xero's trial balance export.

After entering opening balances, run a trial balance in Relentify and compare it to your Xero trial balance. The totals must match exactly. If they do not, identify and correct any discrepancies before proceeding.

## Step 6: Import outstanding invoices and bills

For any unpaid invoices and bills that were outstanding when you left Xero, you have two options:

**Option A: Enter them as opening balance items.** Include the total outstanding receivables and payables in your opening balance entries. This is simpler but means you lose the individual invoice detail in Relentify.

**Option B: Recreate them in Relentify.** Create the outstanding invoices and bills in Relentify with their original dates and amounts. This preserves the detail and allows you to track payment against specific invoices.

For most businesses, Option B is worth the extra effort because it maintains a complete audit trail.

## Step 7: Connect bank feeds

If you use Open Banking, connect your bank accounts in Relentify to start receiving automatic bank feeds. Transactions from the migration date forward will appear automatically.

For transactions before the migration date, your Xero records serve as the historical archive.

## Step 8: Set up recurring transactions

If you had recurring invoices, bills, or journal entries in Xero, set these up in Relentify. Review your Xero repeating transactions list and recreate them.

## Step 9: Register for MTD in Relentify

If you were submitting VAT returns through Xero, you need to authorise Relentify with HMRC for Making Tax Digital. This is a simple process through the Relentify settings that involves signing in to your HMRC Government Gateway account.

Note that you can only have one MTD-authorised software provider at a time, so authorising Relentify will deauthorise Xero.

## Step 10: Verify everything

Run the following reports in Relentify and compare them to your saved Xero reports:

- Trial balance: totals must match
- Aged receivables: outstanding invoices must match
- Aged payables: outstanding bills must match
- Bank account balances: must match your actual bank balances

If everything reconciles, your migration is complete.

## Common pitfalls

**Not reconciling Xero before exporting.** Unreconciled transactions create discrepancies that are difficult to trace after migration. Always reconcile fully before exporting.

**Migrating mid-VAT period.** This creates complexity around which platform covers which portion of the VAT period. If possible, migrate at a VAT period boundary.

**Forgetting recurring transactions.** It is easy to overlook recurring invoices or bills that were set up in Xero months or years ago. Review the full list before deactivating your Xero account.

**Not saving Xero reports.** Once you cancel your Xero subscription, you lose access to historical reports. Save PDF copies of all key reports before cancelling.

## After migration

Keep your Xero account active for at least one month after migration, in read-only mode if possible. This gives you a reference point if you need to check historical data during the transition period.

Once you are confident that Relentify is handling everything correctly, you can cancel your Xero subscription. Your exported data and saved reports serve as your permanent archive.

## How long does it take?

For a typical small business with a standard chart of accounts, fewer than 500 customers, and fewer than 100 outstanding invoices, the entire migration process takes two to four hours. Larger or more complex businesses may take a full day.

The effort is front-loaded — once the migration is complete, you immediately benefit from Relentify's more generous feature set and lower pricing.
