# Relentify vs Monday.com: Project Management and Beyond

When small businesses look for software to manage their operations, they often face a choice between specialised business platforms and flexible project management tools. Monday.com is one of the most popular work management platforms, used by teams worldwide to organise projects, track tasks, and collaborate. Relentify is a purpose-built business platform that combines accounting, CRM, timesheets, inventories, and communication tools in a single system.

These are fundamentally different products with different philosophies. Understanding what each does well — and where each falls short — helps you choose the right fit for your business.

## What They Are

**Monday.com** is a work operating system (Work OS) — a flexible platform where you build boards, automations, and workflows to manage virtually anything. It is used for project management, task tracking, team collaboration, CRM pipelines, marketing planning, and dozens of other use cases. Its strength is flexibility; its weakness is that you build everything yourself from generic components.

**Relentify** is a business management platform with purpose-built modules for [accounting](https://relentify.com/features/accounting), [CRM](https://relentify.com/features/crm), [property inventories](https://relentify.com/features/inventory), [timesheets](https://relentify.com/features/timesheets), [live chat](https://relentify.com/features/chat), and [helpdesk](https://relentify.com/features/connect). Each module is designed for its specific function with the workflows, data structures, and reporting that the function requires.

## Project and Task Management

**Monday.com** excels here. Customisable boards with multiple views (Kanban, Gantt, timeline, calendar, table), automations, dependencies, and integrations make it a powerful project management tool. For teams managing complex projects with multiple stages, dependencies, and contributors, Monday.com provides excellent visibility and control.

**Relentify** is not a project management tool. While it includes task management within specific modules (timesheet-tracked projects, CRM pipeline stages, helpdesk ticket workflows), it does not offer the general-purpose project management capabilities that Monday.com provides.

If your primary need is project management, Monday.com is the stronger choice.

## Accounting and Finance

**Monday.com** does not include accounting functionality. You cannot raise invoices, record expenses, reconcile bank accounts, run payroll, or produce financial statements within Monday.com. You would need separate accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage) and an integration to connect financial data to your Monday.com boards.

**Relentify** includes a full accounting module — double-entry bookkeeping, invoicing, expense management, bank reconciliation, VAT returns, and financial reporting. For businesses that want their financial management integrated with their CRM, timesheets, and other operational tools, this is a significant advantage.

If accounting is a core requirement, Relentify eliminates the need for a separate accounting tool. Monday.com requires one.

## CRM and Customer Relationships

**Monday.com** includes a CRM product (Monday Sales CRM) that provides pipeline management, contact tracking, deal management, and email integration. It is competent for basic CRM needs and benefits from Monday.com's flexible board structure, which lets you customise the CRM to match your sales process.

**Relentify** offers CRM designed for property businesses and service companies, with features like property portfolio management, tenancy tracking, and landlord relationship management alongside standard CRM capabilities (contact management, pipeline stages, communication history).

For general CRM use, both are capable. For property-specific CRM, Relentify has a clear advantage. For sales-heavy businesses that need advanced pipeline analytics and email automation, Monday.com's CRM may be more feature-rich.

## Timesheets and Workforce Management

**Monday.com** offers time tracking as a column type that can be added to any board. It provides basic tracking capabilities — start/stop timers, manual hour entry, and simple reporting. For lightweight time tracking alongside project management, it works.

**Relentify** provides a dedicated [timesheet module](https://relentify.com/features/timesheets) with clock-in/clock-out, GPS verification, shift scheduling, break tracking, overtime calculations, approval workflows, and integration with payroll and accounting. For businesses where time tracking is operationally important (service businesses, agencies, hospitality, construction), the dedicated module is significantly more capable.

## Communication Tools

**Monday.com** focuses on internal team communication — comments, updates, file sharing, and notifications within boards. It integrates with external communication tools (Slack, email) but does not provide customer-facing communication channels.

**Relentify** includes [live chat](https://relentify.com/features/chat) for customer-facing communication and a [helpdesk/connect module](https://relentify.com/features/connect) for managing multi-channel customer support. For businesses that need to communicate with customers through chat, email, and messaging channels, having these tools integrated with CRM and accounting data provides context that standalone communication tools lack.

## Flexibility vs Purpose

This is the fundamental trade-off between the two platforms.

**Monday.com** is extremely flexible. You can build boards and workflows for almost anything — project management, inventory tracking, content calendars, hiring pipelines, event planning. If you need a tool that adapts to unconventional workflows, Monday.com's flexibility is powerful.

The downside of flexibility is complexity. Building effective workflows in Monday.com requires time, thought, and ongoing maintenance. A CRM built on Monday.com boards will never have the depth of a purpose-built CRM. An expense tracker built on Monday.com will not generate VAT returns or reconcile with your bank account.

**Relentify** is purpose-built. Each module does one thing well and integrates with the others. The trade-off is that you cannot customise it into something it was not designed to be. If your business needs a content calendar, event management tool, or product development tracker, Relentify does not provide these.

## Integration

**Monday.com** offers extensive integrations — hundreds of apps connect through native integrations and third-party connectors. If your workflow requires multiple specialised tools, Monday.com can serve as the hub that connects them.

**Relentify** takes an integrated approach, building core business functions into the platform itself. This reduces the need for external integrations but limits the ecosystem of third-party connections.

## Pricing

**Monday.com** pricing is per-user-per-month, with different tiers offering different features. The basic tier is affordable for small teams, but advanced features (automations, integrations, dashboards) require higher tiers that can become expensive as team size grows.

**Relentify** pricing includes access to all modules — accounting, CRM, inventories, timesheets, chat, and helpdesk. For businesses that would otherwise subscribe to multiple separate tools, the consolidated pricing can represent significant savings.

Compare the total cost of your software stack under each approach: Monday.com plus separate accounting, plus separate CRM or helpdesk tools versus Relentify as an all-in-one platform.

## Which Is Right for You?

**Choose Monday.com if:**

- Your primary need is project management and team collaboration
- You value flexibility and want to build custom workflows
- Your team already uses Monday.com and is productive with it
- You have separate tools for accounting, CRM, and timesheets that you are happy with
- You need an integration hub to connect diverse tools

**Choose Relentify if:**

- You want accounting, CRM, timesheets, and communication in one platform
- You are a property business, service company, or letting agency
- You want to reduce the number of software subscriptions you manage
- You need dedicated timesheet management with payroll integration
- You value out-of-the-box functionality over build-it-yourself flexibility

These platforms serve different needs. Monday.com is a flexible work management platform that can be adapted to many uses. Relentify is a focused business management platform that does specific things well and integrates them tightly. The right choice depends on what your business needs most.
