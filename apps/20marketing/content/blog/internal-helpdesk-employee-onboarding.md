# Using an Internal Helpdesk to Streamline Employee Onboarding

A new employee's first week shapes their perception of your organisation for months or years to come. When their laptop is ready on day one, their email works, their security badge grants access, and their desk has everything they need, the message is clear: this is a well-run organisation that values its people. When they spend their first three days chasing IT for a password, waiting for facilities to find them a chair, and discovering that nobody told finance to set up their payroll, the message is equally clear — and much harder to recover from.

The problem is not that people do not care. It is that onboarding involves dozens of tasks across multiple departments, and without a system to coordinate them, things inevitably fall through the cracks. An internal helpdesk provides that system.

## The Onboarding Coordination Problem

Think about everything that needs to happen when a new employee joins:

**IT:** Laptop provisioned, email account created, software installed, VPN access configured, security credentials issued, phone set up, access to shared drives and systems granted.

**HR:** Contract signed, employee record created, payroll set up, benefits enrolled, emergency contacts collected, policies acknowledged, induction scheduled.

**Facilities:** Desk allocated, building access card issued, parking arranged, kitchen and facilities tour scheduled, health and safety briefing completed.

**Department:** Manager briefed, team notified, training plan created, buddy assigned, first-week meetings scheduled, project access granted.

**Finance:** Expense account set up, corporate card issued (if applicable), budget codes assigned, [accounting system access](https://relentify.com/features/accounting) configured.

In a small business, this might be 20-30 individual tasks. In a larger organisation, it can be 50 or more. Each task has a different owner, a different timeline, and a different dependency. Without coordination, the result is a chaotic first week for the new starter and frustrated teams who are constantly chasing each other.

## How a Helpdesk Solves This

An internal [helpdesk system](https://relentify.com/features/connect) transforms onboarding from an informal, email-based process into a structured, trackable workflow.

### Automated Task Creation

When HR confirms a new starter, a single trigger creates all the onboarding tickets automatically. Each ticket is assigned to the correct team with the correct priority and deadline.

IT receives tickets for laptop setup, email creation, and system access — each with the new starter's details, role, department, and start date pre-populated. Facilities receives tickets for desk allocation and building access. Finance receives tickets for payroll and expense setup.

No one needs to remember to tell IT. No one needs to send a chain of emails to multiple departments. The system handles it.

### Dependency Management

Some onboarding tasks depend on others. You cannot grant system access until the laptop is provisioned. You cannot set up payroll until the contract is signed. You cannot schedule the building tour until the access card is issued.

A helpdesk system manages these dependencies — holding dependent tickets until their prerequisites are completed, then automatically assigning them to the next team in the chain.

### Visibility and Tracking

At any point before the new starter's first day, the hiring manager (or HR) can check the helpdesk dashboard and see exactly which onboarding tasks are complete, in progress, or overdue. If IT is behind on laptop provisioning, that is visible a week before the start date — not discovered at 9am on day one.

This visibility enables proactive management. Overdue tasks generate alerts. Bottlenecks are identified and resolved before they affect the new starter's experience.

### Standardisation

Every new starter goes through the same process. The tasks, timelines, and quality standards are consistent regardless of which department they join or which individual handles each task.

This standardisation also makes it easy to improve the process over time. If a particular task consistently runs late, you can investigate the root cause and fix it. If a step is redundant, you can remove it. The data from the helpdesk system shows you exactly where the process works well and where it does not.

## Building Your Onboarding Workflow

### Step 1: Map the Current Process

Document every task that needs to happen when someone joins. Interview each department to ensure nothing is missed. Common categories include:

- Pre-arrival tasks (before day one)
- Day-one essentials (must be ready when they walk in)
- First-week tasks (completed during the first week)
- First-month tasks (probation setup, training completion)

### Step 2: Assign Ownership and Timelines

For each task, identify who is responsible and when it needs to be completed relative to the start date.

Examples:
- Laptop provisioned: IT, 3 days before start date
- Email and system access: IT, 1 day before start date
- Desk and equipment: Facilities, 2 days before start date
- Payroll setup: Finance, 5 days before start date
- Welcome email to team: Manager, 1 day before start date
- Induction session: HR, day one
- Building tour: Facilities, day one
- IT equipment walkthrough: IT, day one

### Step 3: Create Ticket Templates

Build ticket templates for each onboarding task. Each template should include:

- Task description (what needs to be done)
- Required information (new starter name, role, department, start date, equipment requirements)
- Deadline (relative to start date)
- Assigned team or individual
- Dependencies (which tasks must be completed first)
- Completion criteria (how the assignee confirms it is done)

### Step 4: Build the Automation

Configure your helpdesk to create all tickets automatically when triggered. The trigger might be:

- HR entering a new starter record
- A manager submitting an onboarding request form
- An approved offer letter in your HR system

The automation should populate each ticket with the new starter's details and calculate deadlines based on the start date.

### Step 5: Test and Refine

Run the workflow for the next few hires and gather feedback from everyone involved — the new starters, the teams processing the tickets, and the hiring managers. Adjust timelines, add missing tasks, and improve templates based on real experience.

## Beyond Day One

The best onboarding extends well beyond the first day. Use your helpdesk to manage the entire onboarding journey:

**Week one review:** Schedule a ticket for the manager to conduct a first-week check-in. Did everything work? Are there any outstanding issues?

**30-day check-in:** A ticket prompting HR or the manager to review how the new starter is settling in, whether training is on track, and whether any additional support is needed.

**Probation milestones:** Tickets at 30, 60, and 90 days for probation reviews, training completion, and access reviews.

**Equipment audit:** A 30-day ticket for IT to verify that all systems are working correctly and the new starter has everything they need.

This extended onboarding workflow ensures that the new starter continues to receive structured support after the initial excitement fades — the period when many new hires quietly start to disengage if things are not going well.

## Offboarding Too

The same helpdesk approach works in reverse for employee departures. When someone leaves, create automatic tickets for:

- System access revocation (IT)
- Equipment return (IT)
- Building access deactivation (Facilities)
- Final pay processing (Finance)
- Exit interview scheduling (HR)
- Knowledge transfer tasks (Department)

Offboarding is security-critical — a former employee with active system access is a data protection risk. Automated helpdesk tickets ensure nothing is missed.

## Measuring Onboarding Effectiveness

Use your helpdesk data to continuously improve the onboarding process:

- **Time to completion:** How long does the full onboarding process take? Is it getting faster?
- **On-time rate:** What percentage of tasks are completed by their deadline?
- **Bottleneck identification:** Which tasks or teams consistently run late?
- **New starter feedback:** Survey new starters about their onboarding experience
- **First-year retention:** Do employees who had smooth onboarding stay longer?

A structured, helpdesk-driven onboarding process is one of the highest-impact investments a growing business can make. It creates a positive first impression, reduces administrative chaos, ensures compliance and security, and ultimately helps new employees become productive and engaged team members faster.
