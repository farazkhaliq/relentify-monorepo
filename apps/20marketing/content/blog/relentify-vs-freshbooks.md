FreshBooks carved out a niche as the accounting platform for freelancers and small service businesses. Its invoicing is polished, its interface is friendly, and it does a good job of making accounting feel less intimidating for people who are not accountants. But FreshBooks has a pricing model that penalises growth, and its feature set thins out quickly once you need anything beyond basic invoicing and time tracking.

Relentify takes a different approach: offer a comprehensive feature set on every plan and charge less as you grow, not more.

## Pricing comparison

FreshBooks prices its plans based on the number of billable clients. This seems reasonable until your client list grows and you realise you are paying substantially more for the same core features.

| Feature | Relentify (Small Business) | FreshBooks (Plus) |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price | £19/month | £27/month |
| Billable clients | Unlimited | 50 |
| Users | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Invoices | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Expense tracking | Included | Included |
| Mileage tracking | Included | Plus plan only |
| Purchase orders | Included | Not available |
| Multi-entity | Included | Not available |
| Double-entry accounting | Full | Full |

FreshBooks' Lite plan limits you to 5 billable clients for £13 per month. That is fine if you are a freelancer with a handful of regular clients, but the moment you take on a sixth client, you are bumped to the Plus plan at £27 per month. Relentify never limits the number of clients on any plan.

## Invoicing

This is FreshBooks' strongest area, and credit is due — FreshBooks invoicing is well-designed. The invoice creation flow is intuitive, templates look professional, and automated payment reminders work reliably.

Relentify matches FreshBooks on invoicing quality while adding features that FreshBooks lacks. You can convert quotes to invoices in one click, manage credit notes directly from the invoicing workflow, and set up complex recurring invoice schedules. Partial payment recording is built into the invoice view rather than requiring a separate payment entry screen.

Both platforms support online payment acceptance, though FreshBooks charges a transaction fee through its own payment processor while Relentify integrates with Stripe, letting you use your existing merchant rates.

## Expense management

FreshBooks includes basic expense tracking on all plans, with receipt capture and bank connections. Mileage tracking is only available on the Plus plan and above.

Relentify includes expense claims, mileage tracking at current HMRC rates, and approval workflows on every paid plan. The approval workflow is particularly useful for businesses with multiple team members submitting expenses — something FreshBooks does not handle well because its expense system was designed for single-user scenarios.

## Reporting

This is where FreshBooks shows its freelancer-focused roots. The reporting suite covers profit and loss, tax summaries, and expense reports, but it lacks the depth that growing businesses need.

| Report | Relentify | FreshBooks |
|---|---|---|
| Profit & Loss | Yes | Yes |
| Balance Sheet | Yes | Yes |
| Cash Flow Statement | Yes | Limited |
| Trial Balance | Yes | No |
| Aged Receivables | Yes | Yes |
| Aged Payables | Yes | No |
| General Ledger | Yes | No |
| Cash Flow Forecast | Yes | No |
| Custom Reports | Yes | No |
| KPI Dashboard | Yes | No |

FreshBooks does not offer a trial balance, general ledger, or aged payables report. If your accountant needs these for year-end preparation, you will either need to export data to a spreadsheet or switch platforms entirely.

Relentify includes all standard accounting reports plus cash flow forecasting, financial health scores, and the ability to create custom reports without leaving the platform.

## Double-entry accounting

FreshBooks added full double-entry accounting a few years ago after years of operating as a single-entry system. While this was a welcome addition, some accountants feel that the implementation still carries traces of the original single-entry architecture. Journal entries are supported but the workflow is less intuitive than in platforms built around double-entry from the start.

Relentify was built as a double-entry system from day one. Journal entries, account reconciliation, and the chart of accounts all follow standard accounting conventions. Accountants will find the general ledger and trial balance familiar and complete.

## Project tracking and time tracking

FreshBooks includes time tracking as a core feature, which makes sense given its focus on service businesses that bill by the hour. The time tracking interface is simple and effective.

Relentify also includes project tracking with time and expense allocation, project profitability reports, and the ability to invoice directly from tracked time. Where Relentify goes further is in project-level reporting — you can see at a glance which projects are profitable and which are costing more than they earn.

## Multi-entity and growth features

FreshBooks does not support managing multiple businesses from a single account. If you have a consultancy and a product business, you need two separate FreshBooks subscriptions.

Relentify supports multiple entities on one subscription with consolidated reporting, intercompany transactions, and instant entity switching. This makes it a natural fit for businesses that are growing beyond a single entity.

## Purchase orders

FreshBooks does not offer purchase order management. If you need to issue purchase orders to suppliers, track their status, and convert them to bills on receipt, you will need a separate tool.

Relentify includes purchase orders on every paid plan with approval workflows and automatic conversion to bills.

## VAT compliance

Both platforms support MTD-compliant VAT returns. FreshBooks handles standard VAT well for its target market of freelancers and micro-businesses. Relentify goes further with support for the flat rate scheme, reverse charge VAT, and partial exemption.

## User interface

FreshBooks has one of the friendliest interfaces in accounting software. It was designed to be approachable for people who find accounting intimidating, and it succeeds at this goal.

Relentify takes a similar approach to usability but does not sacrifice depth for simplicity. The interface is clean and modern, but it does not hide features behind layers of menus to appear simpler. Every feature is accessible from an intuitive navigation structure.

## Mobile experience

FreshBooks has a well-regarded mobile app for iOS and Android that covers invoicing, expense capture, and time tracking. Relentify's mobile experience is built as a responsive web application that works on any device, with a dedicated app for receipt capture and expense submission.

## Who should choose which

**Choose FreshBooks if** you are a solo freelancer with fewer than five clients who primarily needs invoicing and time tracking, and you do not anticipate growing beyond that.

**Choose Relentify if** you need more than basic invoicing, want unlimited clients without tier-based pricing, need reporting that your accountant will actually use, or plan to grow your business beyond a single entity.

## The verdict

FreshBooks does a few things very well: invoicing, time tracking, and making accounting approachable for non-accountants. But its client-based pricing model, limited reporting, and lack of growth features like multi-entity management and purchase orders mean that many businesses outgrow it quickly.

Relentify offers everything FreshBooks does plus a comprehensive accounting feature set, unlimited clients on every plan, and a price point that stays reasonable as your business grows. If FreshBooks is starting to feel like a tool you have outgrown, Relentify is designed to be the platform you grow into.
