FreeAgent is a UK-focused accounting platform that has built a loyal following among freelancers, sole traders, and small limited companies. It is owned by NatWest Group, which means NatWest business banking customers can access it for free — a significant draw. For everyone else, FreeAgent costs £29 per month.

The platform does several things well: its self-assessment tax return feature is genuinely useful, its interface is approachable, and it handles the basics of UK small business accounting competently. But it also has notable limitations that become frustrating as businesses grow.

Relentify offers more features across every plan tier, at a lower price for most users, without the limitations that make FreeAgent feel constraining.

## Pricing comparison

FreeAgent has a single pricing tier at £29 per month (or free for NatWest/Royal Bank of Scotland business banking customers). There are no plan choices — you either pay £29 or you get it free through your bank.

| Feature | Relentify (Small Business) | FreeAgent |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price | £19/month | £29/month |
| Users | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Invoices | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Expense tracking | Included | Included |
| Mileage tracking | Included | Included |
| Purchase orders | Included | Not available |
| Multi-currency | Included | Limited |
| Multi-entity | Included | Not available |
| Project tracking | Included | Basic |
| Approval workflows | Included | Not available |

Relentify's Small Business plan at £19 per month includes features that FreeAgent does not offer at any price: purchase orders, approval workflows, and multi-entity management. For businesses not banking with NatWest, Relentify saves £10 per month while delivering more.

## Invoicing

FreeAgent's invoicing is solid. You can create professional invoices, set up recurring billing, and send payment reminders. The invoice templates look clean, and the process of creating an invoice is straightforward.

Relentify matches FreeAgent's invoicing quality and adds quote-to-invoice conversion, credit note management within the invoicing workflow, partial payment recording, and document attachments on invoices. The overall invoicing flow involves fewer steps.

## Expense management

FreeAgent includes expense tracking with receipt capture, mileage tracking with HMRC rates, and bank feed categorisation. This is well-implemented and covers the needs of most sole traders and freelancers.

Relentify includes the same expense tracking features plus approval workflows for team expense claims. When your business grows beyond a single person and you need others to submit expenses for approval, Relentify handles this natively while FreeAgent does not.

## Tax features

This is FreeAgent's standout feature. The self-assessment tax estimation tool shows your projected tax liability in real time, including income tax, National Insurance, and corporation tax for limited companies. This is genuinely useful for freelancers who want visibility into their tax position throughout the year.

FreeAgent also supports MTD for VAT with direct HMRC submission and handles the CIS scheme for construction industry workers.

Relentify supports MTD for VAT, CIS, flat rate VAT, and reverse charge VAT. It does not currently offer the same real-time self-assessment tax estimation that FreeAgent provides, though VAT return preparation and submission are fully supported.

For sole traders whose primary concern is self-assessment visibility, FreeAgent's tax feature is a genuine differentiator. For limited companies focused on VAT compliance, the platforms are comparable.

## Reporting

FreeAgent offers a set of reports tailored to its target market: profit and loss, balance sheet, tax summary, and aged debtors. The reports are clear and well-presented but limited in scope.

| Report | Relentify | FreeAgent |
|---|---|---|
| Profit & Loss | Yes | Yes |
| Balance Sheet | Yes | Yes |
| Cash Flow Statement | Yes | Limited |
| Trial Balance | Yes | Yes |
| General Ledger | Yes | No |
| Aged Receivables | Yes | Yes |
| Aged Payables | Yes | Limited |
| Cash Flow Forecast | Yes | No |
| Consolidated Reports | Yes | No |
| Custom Reports | Yes | No |
| KPI Dashboard | Yes | No |

FreeAgent does not offer a general ledger report, cash flow forecast, consolidated reports, or custom reports. Accountants preparing year-end accounts often need to export data from FreeAgent and work with it in spreadsheets.

Relentify includes all standard accounting reports plus advanced reporting features that make year-end preparation easier for both business owners and accountants.

## Multi-entity management

FreeAgent does not support multiple entities. Each business requires its own FreeAgent account at £29 per month (unless you qualify for the NatWest free tier). There is no consolidated view across entities.

Relentify supports multiple entities on a single subscription with consolidated reporting and intercompany transaction management.

## Purchase orders

FreeAgent does not offer purchase order management. For businesses that need to issue purchase orders to suppliers, track their status, and manage approval workflows, a separate tool is required.

Relentify includes purchase orders on every paid plan with approval workflows and automatic conversion to bills.

## Project tracking

FreeAgent includes basic project tracking with time tracking and the ability to allocate hours to projects. This works for freelancers tracking billable time but lacks depth for businesses managing multiple projects with budgets and profitability targets.

Relentify includes project tracking with profitability analysis, expense allocation, and project-specific reporting on every paid plan.

## Bank feeds

FreeAgent supports automatic bank feeds from major UK banks. The transaction categorisation interface is clear and includes the ability to set up rules for recurring transactions.

Relentify also supports automatic bank feeds with Open Banking connectivity, transaction matching, and a learning rule engine for categorisation.

## User interface

FreeAgent has a friendly, approachable interface that works well for its target audience of non-accountants. The navigation is simple, and common tasks are accessible quickly. The design has been consistent over the years, which means existing users feel comfortable.

Relentify's interface is equally approachable but offers more depth. The navigation adapts to the features you use, and the design is modern without sacrificing usability. For businesses growing beyond the basics, Relentify's interface scales better because it was designed to accommodate a broader feature set.

## The NatWest factor

FreeAgent's free tier through NatWest is a significant consideration. If you bank with NatWest or Royal Bank of Scotland and your accounting needs align with what FreeAgent offers, the free access is a genuine benefit.

However, choosing your accounting software based on your banking relationship means you are locked into that bank to maintain free access. If you switch banks, you lose your free FreeAgent subscription. And if FreeAgent's limitations are already frustrating you, the "free" price tag does not eliminate those frustrations — it just makes them cheaper to tolerate.

## Who should choose which

**Choose FreeAgent if** you bank with NatWest and get it for free, your business is a sole trader primarily concerned with self-assessment tax visibility, and you do not need purchase orders, approval workflows, or multi-entity management.

**Choose Relentify if** you want more features at a lower price (£19 vs £29), you need purchase orders or approval workflows, you manage multiple entities, you need comprehensive reporting for your accountant, or you want a platform that grows with your business.

## The bottom line

FreeAgent is a good product for its specific niche: UK freelancers and sole traders who want approachable accounting with self-assessment tax visibility. Its NatWest partnership makes it effectively free for a large segment of the market.

But for businesses that need anything beyond the basics — purchase orders, expense approval workflows, multi-entity management, or advanced reporting — FreeAgent falls short. Relentify offers a broader feature set at a lower price, making it the better choice for businesses that have outgrown freelancer-focused tools or want a platform they will not need to replace as they grow.
