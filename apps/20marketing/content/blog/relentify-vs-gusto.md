# Relentify vs Gusto: Comparing HR, Payroll, and Business Management

Gusto has become one of the most popular payroll and HR platforms for small businesses in the United States, known for its user-friendly interface and comprehensive payroll features. Relentify takes a different approach, offering a broader business management platform that combines accounting, CRM, timesheets, and communication tools. These platforms overlap in workforce management but differ significantly in scope and focus.

This comparison helps you understand what each platform does best and which serves your business needs more effectively.

## What They Are

**Gusto** is a payroll-first platform that has expanded into HR, benefits administration, and workforce management. It handles payroll processing, tax filing, benefits enrolment, time tracking, hiring and onboarding, and compliance — all focused on the employer-employee relationship. Gusto is built primarily for the US market, with deep integration into US tax and benefits systems.

**Relentify** is a business management platform with modules for [accounting](https://relentify.com/features/accounting), [CRM](https://relentify.com/features/crm), [timesheets](https://relentify.com/features/timesheets), [property inventories](https://relentify.com/features/inventory), [live chat](https://relentify.com/features/chat), and [helpdesk](https://relentify.com/features/connect). It serves businesses that need operational tools beyond just payroll and HR, including customer-facing functions and financial management.

## Payroll

**Gusto** is exceptionally strong here. It handles full payroll processing — salary calculations, tax withholdings (federal, state, and local), direct deposits, tax filings, W-2 and 1099 generation, and year-end reporting. Gusto automatically files payroll taxes and handles compliance updates when tax rates change. For US payroll, it is one of the best solutions available for small businesses.

Gusto also supports contractor payments, multi-state payroll, and international contractor payments through its global capabilities.

**Relentify** does not currently offer full US payroll processing with automated tax filing. Its [timesheet module](https://relentify.com/features/timesheets) tracks hours, manages shifts, and calculates overtime, providing the data that feeds into payroll — but the actual payroll processing would need a dedicated tool or integration.

For US payroll, Gusto has a clear advantage.

## HR and People Management

**Gusto** provides a solid HR toolkit:

- Employee self-service portal for pay stubs, tax documents, and benefits
- Hiring and onboarding workflows (offer letters, I-9 and W-4 collection, background checks)
- Benefits administration (health insurance, 401k, HSA, FSA, commuter benefits)
- PTO tracking and management
- Performance reviews (in higher tiers)
- Compliance alerts and document storage

For small businesses that need a single system to manage the entire employee lifecycle from hiring through payroll to benefits, Gusto delivers a cohesive experience.

**Relentify** approaches workforce management differently — focusing on operational workforce tools (timesheets, shift scheduling, clock-in/out) rather than HR administration. It does not currently manage benefits enrolment, generate tax documents, or provide the hiring workflow tools that Gusto offers.

## Time Tracking and Timesheets

**Gusto** includes basic time tracking that integrates directly with payroll. Employees can clock in and out through the Gusto app, managers approve timesheets, and approved hours flow automatically into payroll calculations. For businesses with straightforward time tracking needs, this integration is seamless.

**Relentify** offers more robust timesheet functionality — GPS-verified clock-in/out, shift scheduling, break management, multi-site tracking, overtime calculations, and detailed reporting. For businesses where time tracking is operationally complex (multiple locations, mobile workforces, variable shifts, detailed project tracking), Relentify's [timesheet module](https://relentify.com/features/timesheets) provides greater depth.

If your time tracking needs are simple and payroll integration is the priority, Gusto's built-in tracking works well. If time tracking is a core operational function, Relentify offers more capability.

## Accounting and Financial Management

**Gusto** does not include accounting software. It integrates with popular accounting platforms (QuickBooks, Xero, FreshBooks) to sync payroll data with your books, but you need a separate accounting subscription and integration setup.

**Relentify** includes full [accounting](https://relentify.com/features/accounting) — invoicing, expense management, bank reconciliation, financial reporting, and tax preparation support. For businesses that want financial management and workforce management in one system, this integration eliminates the need for a separate accounting tool and the integration complexity that comes with it.

## CRM and Customer Management

**Gusto** is entirely focused on the employer side of the business. It does not include CRM, customer management, or sales pipeline tools.

**Relentify** includes a [CRM module](https://relentify.com/features/crm) for managing customer relationships, sales pipelines, and communication history. For businesses where customer relationship management is important (service companies, property businesses, agencies), having CRM alongside accounting and timesheets provides a unified view of business operations.

## Communication Tools

**Gusto** communicates with employees through its platform — payroll notifications, benefits information, and HR communications. It does not provide customer-facing communication tools.

**Relentify** includes [live chat](https://relentify.com/features/chat) and [helpdesk](https://relentify.com/features/connect) tools for customer-facing communication. These are not HR tools — they serve the customer support and engagement side of the business.

## Market Focus

**Gusto** is built for the US market. Its payroll tax engine, benefits marketplace, and compliance tools are deeply integrated with US federal and state systems. If your business operates in the US and needs US-compliant payroll and HR, Gusto is purpose-built for you.

**Relentify** serves an international market with features that work across jurisdictions. Its accounting module supports multiple tax systems and currencies. This broader focus means it does not offer the depth of US-specific compliance that Gusto provides, but it serves businesses operating in multiple countries or outside the US.

## Pricing

**Gusto** pricing is per-employee-per-month plus a base fee. Plans range from basic payroll to full-service HR with benefits administration. The cost scales with headcount, which can become significant for larger teams.

**Relentify** pricing includes access to all modules. For businesses that would need Gusto plus separate accounting software plus CRM plus other operational tools, the total cost comparison favours the all-in-one approach.

Calculate your total software spend under each scenario. If Gusto is your payroll solution, add the cost of accounting, CRM, time tracking (if Gusto's built-in tracking is insufficient), and any other tools you need. Compare that total to Relentify's subscription.

## Which Is Right for You?

**Choose Gusto if:**

- You are a US-based business and need automated US payroll with tax filing
- Benefits administration (health insurance, 401k) is a priority
- You want a polished, end-to-end HR platform
- You already have accounting and CRM solutions that you are happy with
- Payroll compliance and automation are your primary concerns

**Choose Relentify if:**

- You need accounting, CRM, timesheets, and communication tools in one platform
- You operate outside the US or in multiple countries
- Advanced time tracking and shift management are important
- Customer-facing tools (chat, helpdesk) are part of your requirements
- You want to consolidate your software stack and reduce the number of subscriptions

For US small businesses with straightforward needs, Gusto excels at what it does — payroll and HR. For businesses that need a broader operational platform that includes financial management, customer relationships, and workforce tools, Relentify offers a more comprehensive solution. Many businesses may find value in using both — Gusto for US payroll and Relentify for everything else.
