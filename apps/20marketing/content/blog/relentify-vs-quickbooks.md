QuickBooks by Intuit is the most widely used small business accounting software in the world. Its market dominance is undeniable, built on decades of brand recognition and a massive ecosystem of accountants trained on the platform. But market share does not always translate to best value, and many QuickBooks users are discovering that they are paying premium prices for a platform that has not kept pace with modern expectations.

Relentify was built as a ground-up alternative that addresses the specific frustrations QuickBooks users face: opaque pricing, feature restrictions on lower tiers, and an interface that often feels more complex than it needs to be.

## Pricing comparison

QuickBooks Online has four tiers in the UK: Simple Start, Essentials, Plus, and Advanced. The pricing has increased significantly over the past few years, and many features that feel essential are only available on higher plans.

| Feature | Relentify (Growth) | QuickBooks (Plus) |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price | £29/month | £35/month |
| Users included | Unlimited | 5 |
| Invoices | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Bill management | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Project tracking | Included | Plus plan only |
| Multi-currency | Included | Plus plan only |
| Purchase orders | Included | Plus plan only |
| Inventory tracking | Included | Plus plan only |
| Additional user cost | Free | £11/user/month |

The user seat model is where QuickBooks becomes particularly expensive. QuickBooks Plus includes five users, and each additional user costs £11 per month. For a business with ten team members, that is an extra £55 per month. Relentify includes unlimited users on every paid plan.

## Invoicing and quotes

Both platforms handle basic invoicing competently. You can create professional invoices, set up recurring billing, and send payment reminders.

Where they diverge is in the details. Relentify allows you to convert quotes to invoices in a single click, attach documents to invoices, and record partial payments without navigating away from the invoice screen. The quote-to-invoice workflow in QuickBooks works but involves more steps, and the interface for managing payment reminders requires navigating through multiple settings screens.

Relentify also includes credit note management as a standard feature. Creating, tracking, and applying credit notes is integrated directly into the invoicing workflow rather than being a separate process.

## Expense tracking

QuickBooks includes basic expense tracking on all plans, but the experience varies significantly by tier. Receipt capture via mobile is available, but the categorisation and approval workflow is more manual than it could be.

Relentify includes full expense management with approval workflows, mileage tracking with HMRC-compliant rates, and automated categorisation rules. There is no per-user charge for expense management — it is included in the plan price.

| Feature | Relentify | QuickBooks |
|---|---|---|
| Expense claims | Built-in | Basic on all plans |
| Mileage tracking | Built-in with rates | Requires Plus or above |
| Approval workflows | Built-in | Limited |
| Receipt scanning | Built-in | Built-in |
| Per-user charge | None | Included in user seats |

## Bank feeds and reconciliation

Both platforms offer automatic bank feeds from major UK banks. The reconciliation process is broadly similar — match transactions to invoices, bills, or manual entries.

QuickBooks' reconciliation interface has improved over the years but still involves more clicks than necessary for routine matching. The suggested match algorithm works reasonably well for recurring transactions but can struggle with one-off payments.

Relentify's reconciliation interface was built with speed as the primary design goal. Transactions can be matched, categorised, and confirmed with minimal clicks, and the rule engine learns from your patterns to suggest better matches over time.

## Reporting

QuickBooks offers a solid set of standard reports: profit and loss, balance sheet, cash flow statements, and aged receivables. Custom reports are available on the Plus and Advanced plans.

Relentify includes all standard reports plus cash flow forecasting, financial health scores, KPI dashboards, and consolidated reporting for multi-entity businesses. Custom reports are available on every paid plan, not just higher tiers.

| Report type | Relentify | QuickBooks |
|---|---|---|
| Standard financial reports | All plans | All plans |
| Custom reports | All paid plans | Plus and Advanced |
| Cash flow forecasting | All paid plans | Advanced only |
| Consolidated reports | All paid plans | Not available |
| Financial health score | All paid plans | Not available |

## Multi-entity management

Running multiple businesses is painful with QuickBooks. Each entity requires its own subscription, its own login, and there is no native way to see consolidated reporting across entities.

Relentify supports multiple entities on a single subscription with instant switching, consolidated reporting, and intercompany transaction management. If you manage two or three businesses, this alone could justify the switch.

## VAT and Making Tax Digital

Both platforms are MTD-compliant and can submit VAT returns directly to HMRC. QuickBooks handles standard VAT well, though the flat rate scheme and partial exemption calculations can require manual adjustments.

Relentify automates the nine-box VAT return calculation, supports the flat rate scheme, and handles reverse charge VAT for domestic services. The VAT return preview shows a clear breakdown before submission, reducing the anxiety of clicking "submit" on your return.

## Purchase orders

Purchase orders are only available on QuickBooks Plus and above. On Relentify, they are included on every paid plan with full approval workflows, escalation rules for overdue approvals, and automatic conversion to bills on receipt.

## Project tracking

QuickBooks includes project tracking on its Plus and Advanced plans. It works for basic time and expense allocation to projects but lacks the depth that service businesses often need.

Relentify includes project tracking on every paid plan with the ability to track profitability, allocate expenses, and generate project-specific reports.

## User interface

QuickBooks has gone through several redesigns over the years, and the result is an interface that sometimes feels inconsistent. Some areas have been modernised while others still carry the design language of earlier versions. Navigation can be confusing for new users, with features sometimes appearing in unexpected places.

Relentify benefits from being designed as a single, cohesive application. The navigation is consistent, the design language is uniform, and common tasks are accessible from where you would expect to find them.

## Integrations

QuickBooks has a larger app marketplace than Relentify, which is natural given its longer time in market. If you rely on specific integrations that are only available through QuickBooks, this is worth checking before you switch.

However, many of the integrations QuickBooks users rely on are for features that Relentify includes natively: expense management, purchase order approval, project tracking, and multi-entity management.

## Support

QuickBooks offers phone and chat support, though wait times can be significant, particularly during busy periods like tax season. Support quality can vary, with some users reporting being directed to generic help articles rather than receiving specific guidance.

Relentify provides support through live chat with both AI-assisted responses for common questions and human agents for complex issues. Response times are consistently shorter, and the support team has direct access to your account to diagnose issues quickly.

## Who should choose which

**Choose QuickBooks if** your accountant requires it, you need specific third-party integrations only available through QuickBooks, or you are deeply embedded in the Intuit ecosystem with payroll and other products.

**Choose Relentify if** you want unlimited users without per-seat charges, need multi-entity support on a single subscription, want more features on lower-priced plans, or prefer a modern and consistent user interface.

## Summary

QuickBooks is a capable platform with enormous market share, but that share was built in a different era of software. Its per-user pricing, feature restrictions on lower tiers, and increasingly complex interface mean that many small businesses are paying more than they need to for less than they deserve.

Relentify offers a more generous feature set at a lower price point, with unlimited users, built-in expense management, and multi-entity support included rather than charged as extras. For businesses willing to look beyond the default choice, the numbers speak for themselves.
