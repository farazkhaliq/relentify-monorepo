KashFlow was one of the original UK cloud accounting platforms, launching in 2005 — before Xero, before FreeAgent, and before most of the competition. It was acquired by IRIS Software in 2013, and since then, development has noticeably slowed. The platform still works, but it has not kept pace with what modern businesses expect from their accounting software.

For businesses currently on KashFlow or considering it, Relentify offers a modern alternative with more features, better pricing, and an interface designed for 2026.

## Pricing comparison

KashFlow offers three tiers: Starter, Business, and Business + Payroll. The pricing is competitive on the surface, but the feature limitations on lower tiers push many businesses toward the more expensive plans.

| Feature | Relentify (Small Business) | KashFlow (Business) |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price | £19/month | £23/month |
| Users | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Invoices | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Bank feeds | Included | Included |
| Purchase orders | Included | Included |
| Multi-currency | Included | Not available |
| Expense claims | Included | Limited |
| Approval workflows | Included | Not available |
| Multi-entity | Included | Not available |
| Project tracking | Included | Not available |

Relentify's Small Business plan at £19 per month includes multi-currency, expense approval workflows, multi-entity management, and project tracking — none of which KashFlow offers at any price point.

## The stagnation problem

KashFlow's core issue is not that it is bad software — it is that it has not significantly evolved since its acquisition. The interface looks dated, the feature set has not expanded meaningfully, and integration options are limited compared to competitors.

For businesses that chose KashFlow years ago and have been running on it since, the platform still does its job. But for businesses evaluating options today, the lack of modern features is a significant drawback.

## Invoicing

KashFlow's invoicing covers the basics: create invoices, send them, track payment status, and set up recurring billing. The invoice templates are functional but limited in customisation options, and the overall design feels dated.

Relentify's invoicing offers the same core features with a modern interface, fully customisable templates, quote-to-invoice conversion, credit note management, and automated payment reminders. The invoicing workflow is faster and more intuitive.

## Bank reconciliation

KashFlow supports automatic bank feeds and transaction matching. The reconciliation interface works but has not been updated to match the speed and intuitiveness of more modern platforms.

Relentify's reconciliation is built for speed with one-click matching, rule-based categorisation, and a learning engine that improves suggestions over time.

## Reporting

KashFlow offers standard financial reports: profit and loss, balance sheet, VAT returns, and aged debtors. The reports are functional but offer minimal customisation.

Relentify provides a significantly deeper reporting suite with cash flow forecasting, financial health scores, KPI dashboards, and the ability to create custom reports within the platform.

## Purchase orders

KashFlow includes basic purchase order functionality on its Business plan. You can create and send purchase orders, though the workflow lacks approval mechanisms and automatic conversion to bills.

Relentify includes purchase orders with full approval workflows, escalation rules for overdue approvals, and automatic conversion to bills when goods or services are received.

## VAT compliance

Both platforms support MTD for VAT and can submit returns to HMRC. KashFlow handles standard VAT schemes adequately.

Relentify adds support for the flat rate scheme, reverse charge VAT, and period locking. The VAT return interface provides a clear nine-box breakdown before submission.

## Multi-currency

KashFlow does not support multi-currency accounting. For businesses that invoice or pay suppliers in foreign currencies, this is a significant limitation that requires manual calculations or a separate tool.

Relentify includes multi-currency support on every paid plan with automatic exchange rate updates and currency gain/loss tracking.

## Expense management

KashFlow offers basic expense tracking but lacks a proper expense claims workflow. There are no approval mechanisms, no mileage tracking, and the process of recording expenses is manual.

Relentify includes expense claims with approval workflows, mileage tracking at HMRC rates, and receipt capture on every paid plan.

## Multi-entity management

KashFlow does not support managing multiple entities from a single account. Each business requires its own subscription.

Relentify supports multiple entities on one subscription with instant switching, consolidated reporting, and intercompany transaction management.

## User interface

KashFlow's interface reflects its age. While it is functional, the design has not been updated to match modern expectations. Navigation can feel clunky, page load times are slower than contemporary platforms, and the overall experience feels like software from a different era.

Relentify was built in 2026 with modern UI patterns, responsive design, and a focus on minimising clicks for common tasks.

## Integrations

KashFlow's integration ecosystem is limited compared to larger platforms. It connects with major UK banks for bank feeds and has some integrations with payroll and CRM tools, but the options are fewer than what Xero, QuickBooks, or Relentify offer.

Relentify includes Open Banking connectivity and integrates with common business tools. Many features that KashFlow users might seek through integrations — expense management, project tracking, multi-currency — are built into Relentify natively.

## Support

KashFlow offers email and phone support during UK business hours. Response quality can vary, and some users report that support tickets take longer to resolve than they would like.

Relentify provides live chat support on every plan with AI-assisted responses for common questions and human agents for complex issues.

## Who should choose which

**Choose KashFlow if** you have been using it for years with no issues, your needs are basic (simple invoicing, bank reconciliation, VAT returns), and the switching cost feels too high for minimal gain.

**Choose Relentify if** you want a modern platform with a broader feature set, need multi-currency or multi-entity support, want expense approval workflows and project tracking, or your KashFlow experience is starting to feel outdated.

## Making the switch

Migrating from KashFlow to Relentify involves exporting your data (chart of accounts, customer and supplier records, opening balances) and importing it through Relentify's migration tools. KashFlow supports CSV exports that are compatible with Relentify's import templates.

## The bottom line

KashFlow was a pioneer in UK cloud accounting, and it served its users well for many years. But the platform has not kept pace with what growing businesses need. Its lack of multi-currency, multi-entity management, expense approval workflows, and modern reporting makes it increasingly difficult to justify — especially when modern alternatives offer all of these features at a lower price.

Relentify is the upgrade that KashFlow users have been waiting for. It takes everything KashFlow does well and adds the features that modern businesses need, wrapped in an interface that feels like it belongs in 2026.
