Sage is one of the oldest names in UK accounting software. Founded in 1981, it has served millions of businesses and built deep roots in the accountancy profession. But longevity brings baggage, and Sage's product lineup has become confusing, its pricing opaque, and its interface a patchwork of different design eras.

Relentify offers a clean alternative: one product, transparent pricing, and a modern interface built for how businesses work in 2026.

## Understanding Sage's product lineup

Before comparing features, it is worth understanding Sage's confusing product range. Sage currently offers Sage Accounting (formerly Sage Business Cloud), Sage 50, Sage 200, Sage Intacct, and several other products targeting different market segments.

For this comparison, we are focusing on Sage Accounting — the cloud product most directly comparable to Relentify. Sage 50 is a desktop product with a cloud add-on, and Sage 200 and Intacct target larger enterprises.

## Pricing comparison

Sage Accounting has two tiers: Accounting Start and Accounting. The pricing is straightforward on the surface but becomes less clear when you factor in add-ons and limitations.

| Feature | Relentify (Growth) | Sage Accounting |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price | £29/month | £33/month |
| Invoices | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Cash flow forecasting | Included | Included |
| Purchase orders | Included | Not available |
| Multi-currency | Included | Not available |
| Expense claims | Included | Requires add-on |
| Multi-entity | Included | Separate subscription |
| User seats | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Mileage tracking | Included | Not available |

Sage Accounting does not include purchase orders, multi-currency support, or mileage tracking on its cloud product. These features are available in Sage 50 (the desktop product) but not in the cloud version that most new users are directed toward. This creates a frustrating situation where cloud-first businesses either accept the limitations or are pushed toward a desktop product.

## Invoicing

Both platforms handle standard invoicing well. You can create, customise, and send invoices, set up recurring billing, and track payment status.

Sage's invoicing interface has been modernised in recent years but still carries some design choices from earlier iterations. The workflow for creating a new invoice involves more form fields than necessary, and the template customisation options are limited compared to what many businesses expect in 2026.

Relentify's invoicing is streamlined. Creating an invoice takes fewer steps, templates are fully customisable, and the quote-to-invoice conversion is a single click. Credit note management, partial payment recording, and automated reminders are all integrated into the invoicing workflow.

## Bank reconciliation

Sage Accounting includes automatic bank feeds and a reconciliation interface. The matching algorithm works reasonably well for straightforward transactions, though it can struggle with payments that do not exactly match an invoice amount.

Relentify's reconciliation interface prioritises speed. Transactions are presented one at a time with clear match suggestions, and the rule engine learns from your categorisation patterns to improve suggestions over time.

## Reporting

Sage Accounting offers standard financial reports: profit and loss, balance sheet, and aged debtors and creditors. The cash flow forecast feature is a welcome addition that was introduced in recent years.

However, the reporting depth does not match what businesses with growing complexity need. There is no general ledger report, no trial balance export, and no consolidated reporting for multi-entity businesses.

Relentify includes all standard reports plus cash flow forecasting, general ledger detail, financial health scores, and the ability to build custom reports within the platform.

## Expense management

Sage Accounting does not include built-in expense management. To handle expense claims, you need the Sage Expenses add-on, which adds cost and complexity.

Relentify includes expense claims, mileage tracking, and approval workflows on every paid plan. There are no add-on charges and no per-user fees for expense management.

## VAT and Making Tax Digital

Both platforms are MTD-compliant and support direct VAT return submission to HMRC. Sage has deep experience with UK tax compliance, and its VAT handling is reliable.

Relentify matches Sage's VAT capabilities and adds support for the flat rate scheme, reverse charge VAT, and period locking to prevent accidental changes to submitted periods.

## Multi-entity management

Sage Accounting requires a separate subscription for each business entity. There is no way to view consolidated reports or manage multiple entities from a single dashboard.

Relentify supports multiple entities on one subscription with instant switching, consolidated reporting, and intercompany transaction management.

## Purchase orders

Sage Accounting does not include purchase order management. This is a significant gap for businesses that need to manage procurement workflows. You either accept the limitation, add a third-party tool, or switch to Sage 50 (desktop).

Relentify includes purchase orders on every paid plan with approval workflows, escalation rules, and automatic conversion to bills.

## The Sage 50 question

Many businesses using Sage 50 (the desktop product) are considering a move to the cloud. Sage's own cloud product does not offer feature parity with Sage 50, which creates a dilemma: stay on desktop with more features, or move to cloud with fewer.

Relentify offers a cloud-native alternative that includes many of the features Sage 50 users expect — purchase orders, multi-currency, detailed reporting — without requiring desktop software or a local installation.

## User interface

Sage Accounting's interface has been modernised but still feels like it carries the DNA of earlier versions. Navigation is not always intuitive, and some tasks require more clicks than they should.

Relentify was built from scratch with a modern design framework. The interface is consistent, responsive, and designed to minimise the number of steps required for common tasks.

## Integrations

Sage has a large ecosystem of integrations, particularly in the UK market where it has been the dominant platform for decades. Many payroll providers, CRM tools, and industry-specific applications integrate with Sage.

Relentify's integration ecosystem is smaller but includes the most commonly needed connections. Many features that Sage users access through integrations — expense management, purchase orders, project tracking — are built into Relentify natively.

## Who should choose which

**Choose Sage if** you are deeply embedded in the Sage ecosystem with existing integrations, your accountant requires Sage specifically, or you need Sage 50's desktop features and are not ready for a cloud-only platform.

**Choose Relentify if** you want a cloud-native platform with more features than Sage Accounting offers, need multi-entity management without multiple subscriptions, want expense management included rather than as an add-on, or are a Sage 50 user looking for a cloud alternative that does not require compromising on features.

## The bottom line

Sage built its reputation on reliability and depth, and those qualities still exist in its desktop products. But Sage Accounting — the cloud product — does not match its desktop sibling's feature set, and its pricing does not justify the limitations when compared to modern alternatives.

Relentify offers a more complete cloud accounting experience at a lower price, with features like purchase orders, multi-currency, and expense management included on every plan. For businesses looking to modernise their accounting without accepting feature compromises, the case for switching is clear.
