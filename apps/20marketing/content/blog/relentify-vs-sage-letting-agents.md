# Relentify vs Sage for Letting Agents: Which Platform Fits Your Agency?

Choosing accounting software for a letting agency is not the same as choosing it for a general small business. Letting agents have specific financial management needs — tracking rental income and landlord disbursements, managing client money accounts, reconciling tenant deposits, and producing landlord statements. Generic accounting software handles the basics, but the question is whether it handles the property-specific requirements that make your day-to-day operations efficient.

This comparison looks at how Relentify and Sage each serve letting agents, examining the features that matter most to property management businesses.

## Overview

**Sage** is one of the most established names in accounting software, with a product range spanning from Sage Business Cloud Accounting for small businesses to Sage Intacct for larger enterprises. It has millions of users worldwide and a particularly strong presence among accountants and bookkeepers. Sage is a general-purpose accounting platform that serves businesses across every industry.

**Relentify** is a newer, purpose-built platform designed specifically for property businesses and small-to-medium enterprises that need more than just accounting. It combines [accounting](https://relentify.com/features/accounting), [CRM](https://relentify.com/features/crm), [property inventory management](https://relentify.com/features/inventory), [timesheets](https://relentify.com/features/timesheets), [live chat](https://relentify.com/features/chat), and [helpdesk](https://relentify.com/features/connect) tools in a single integrated platform.

## Accounting Fundamentals

Both platforms handle core accounting functions competently — invoicing, expense tracking, bank reconciliation, VAT returns, and financial reporting. For standard bookkeeping, either platform gets the job done.

**Sage** offers a mature, comprehensive accounting engine with features refined over decades. Chart of accounts management, journal entries, multi-currency support, and advanced reporting are all robust. The reporting suite is particularly strong, with extensive customisation options.

**Relentify** provides a modern accounting interface built on the same fundamental principles but designed with a streamlined user experience. The focus is on making accounting accessible to business owners who are not accountants, while still supporting the full range of double-entry bookkeeping functions that professionals expect.

For pure accounting functionality, Sage's longer heritage gives it depth in edge cases and advanced features. For day-to-day letting agency accounting, both platforms cover the essentials well.

## Property-Specific Features

This is where the comparison becomes more relevant for letting agents.

### Landlord Management

**Sage:** As a general-purpose tool, Sage does not have native landlord management features. You can set up landlords as suppliers and properties as cost centres, but the workflows are not designed around the landlord-agent relationship. Generating landlord statements, tracking management fees, and reconciling rent-to-disbursement flows requires manual configuration or workarounds.

**Relentify:** The CRM module is designed for property businesses. Landlord profiles link to properties, tenancies, and financial records. Tracking communication history, management fees, and landlord relationships is built into the platform rather than bolted on.

### Tenant and Property Tracking

**Sage:** Tenant management is not a native Sage function. You would need to use a separate property management system alongside Sage and ensure data flows between them correctly.

**Relentify:** Tenant records, property portfolios, and tenancy details sit alongside financial records in the same system. When you raise an invoice for a property, the associated landlord, tenant, and property details are all accessible without switching between applications.

### Inventory Reports

**Sage:** No inventory report functionality. You would need a separate inventory management tool.

**Relentify:** The [inventory module](https://relentify.com/features/inventory) provides digital property inventory reports — check-in, check-out, and mid-term inspections — directly within the platform. This is a significant differentiator for letting agents who currently manage inventories through separate software or paper-based processes.

## Integration and Ecosystem

**Sage** has an extensive ecosystem of integrations and add-ons. If you need property management functionality alongside Sage, third-party integrations with tools like Reapit, Goodlord, or Arthur Online can fill the gap. However, integrations add cost, complexity, and potential points of failure. Data flowing between multiple systems needs monitoring and maintenance.

**Relentify** takes the opposite approach — building the tools letting agents need into a single platform. Accounting, CRM, inventories, and communication tools share a single database, eliminating integration headaches and ensuring data consistency. The trade-off is a smaller third-party ecosystem, but for businesses that value simplicity and integration, this is often an advantage.

## Pricing

**Sage** pricing varies by product and region. Sage Business Cloud Accounting starts at a competitive price point for basic bookkeeping and increases with additional features and users. Add the cost of separate property management, CRM, and inventory tools, and the total cost of the technology stack can be significant.

**Relentify** includes accounting, CRM, inventories, timesheets, and communication tools in its subscription. For letting agents who would otherwise need to pay for multiple separate tools, the combined cost may be lower than a Sage-plus-add-ons approach.

Calculate the total cost of ownership for each approach, including all the tools you need — not just the accounting software price.

## Accountant Access

**Sage** has deep accountant integration. Most accounting practices are familiar with Sage, and many use Sage products themselves. Giving your accountant access to Sage is straightforward, and they will likely know the system already.

**Relentify** provides accountant access with multi-user permissions. While your accountant may be less familiar with the interface initially, the accounting fundamentals (chart of accounts, trial balance, journal entries) follow standard conventions. Most accountants adapt quickly to new software.

If your accountant strongly prefers a specific platform, that preference is worth weighing — a good working relationship with your accountant is valuable.

## Support and Training

**Sage** provides phone and chat support, an extensive knowledge base, and a large community forum. The sheer number of Sage users means there are abundant tutorials, courses, and accountant expertise available.

**Relentify** provides direct support with a focus on responsiveness and personal service. As a newer and more focused platform, support interactions tend to reach people with direct product knowledge more quickly.

## Which Is Right for Your Agency?

**Choose Sage if:**

- Your accountant strongly prefers Sage and you value that working relationship
- You already have property management software that integrates well with Sage
- You need advanced accounting features (multi-currency, complex consolidation)
- You prefer the security of an established, widely-used platform

**Choose Relentify if:**

- You want accounting, CRM, inventories, and communication tools in one platform
- You value simplicity and want to reduce the number of separate software subscriptions
- Property-specific features (landlord management, inventory reports) are important to you
- You prefer a modern, integrated experience over a best-of-breed multi-tool approach

The right choice depends on your priorities — depth and ecosystem (Sage) or integration and property focus (Relentify). Both can serve a letting agency well; they just approach the challenge differently.
