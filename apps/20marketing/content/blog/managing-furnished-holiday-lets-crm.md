Furnished holiday lets occupy a unique position in the property market. Unlike standard assured shorthold tenancies, holiday lets involve short stays, high turnover, seasonal demand fluctuations, and a level of guest management that has more in common with hospitality than traditional lettings.

For letting agencies that manage holiday lets alongside their standard portfolio — or for those considering expanding into this market — the operational demands are significantly different. A CRM that supports the specific workflows of holiday let management makes the difference between a profitable service line and an operational headache.

## How holiday lets differ

The fundamental difference is frequency. A standard let involves one tenancy setup per year, perhaps two. A holiday let might involve fifty or more bookings per year, each with its own guest, check-in time, cleaning requirement, and communication sequence.

This frequency creates operational intensity. Every changeover requires cleaning, linen changes, inspection, and guest communication. Every booking requires confirmation, information provision, key access, and follow-up. The margin for error is slim — a guest who arrives at a dirty property or cannot access the keys will leave a negative review that affects future bookings.

## Booking management

The core of holiday let management is the booking calendar. Your system needs to track availability, confirmed bookings, pricing (which varies by season), and guest details for every property.

Most holiday lets are marketed through multiple channels — your own website, booking platforms, and direct enquiries. Managing availability across these channels to prevent double-bookings is essential. A CRM or channel manager that syncs availability in real time across all platforms eliminates this risk.

Each booking record should capture the guest's name and contact details, the arrival and departure dates, the number of guests, the total price, the payment status, and any special requirements.

## Guest communication sequences

Guest communication for holiday lets follows a predictable sequence that lends itself to automation.

**Booking confirmation.** Sent immediately upon booking, confirming the dates, the price, the property address, and the cancellation policy.

**Pre-arrival information.** Sent a few days before arrival, providing directions, check-in instructions, key access details, local area information, and any house rules.

**Welcome message.** Sent on the day of arrival, welcoming the guest and providing a contact number for any issues during their stay.

**Mid-stay check-in.** For longer stays, a brief message checking that everything is satisfactory and offering to help with any questions.

**Post-departure follow-up.** Sent after checkout, thanking the guest for their stay and inviting them to leave a review.

Each of these messages can be templated and triggered automatically by the CRM based on the booking dates. The content is personalised — the guest's name, the property name, specific check-in instructions — but the triggering and sending is automated.

## Changeover management

The changeover — the period between one guest departing and the next arriving — is the most operationally critical window in holiday let management. In peak season, this window might be as short as a few hours.

Your CRM should support a changeover workflow that is triggered automatically when a departure date approaches. The workflow generates tasks for the cleaning team, the linen service, and the property inspector, each with a deadline based on the next guest's arrival time.

Tracking the completion of each task is essential. If the cleaning has not been confirmed by a certain time, an alert should go to the manager. If the inspection reveals an issue — a broken appliance, a stain that will not come out, a missing item — there must be time to address it before the next guest arrives.

## Pricing and revenue optimisation

Holiday let pricing is dynamic. Peak season rates may be two or three times higher than off-peak rates. School holidays, bank holidays, and local events all affect demand and pricing.

Your CRM should support seasonal pricing rules that automatically apply the correct rate based on the booking dates. It should also track revenue per property over time, allowing you to compare performance across your holiday let portfolio and identify properties that are underperforming.

Occupancy rate — the percentage of available nights that are booked — is the key performance metric for holiday lets. Your CRM should calculate this automatically and present it on your dashboard alongside revenue and average nightly rate.

## Maintenance and inventory management

Holiday lets are furnished, which means you are responsible for maintaining not just the property but its contents. Broken crockery, worn towels, malfunctioning appliances — these all need to be identified and replaced promptly.

Your changeover inspection should include an inventory check, with any issues logged in the CRM and replacement or repair tasks generated automatically. Over time, the maintenance and replacement data helps you budget for each property and advise landlords on the true cost of operating a holiday let.

## Guest reviews and reputation management

Reviews are the lifeblood of holiday let marketing. A property with consistently positive reviews attracts more bookings and commands higher prices. A property with negative reviews — even a single one — can see a significant drop in bookings.

Your CRM should track reviews for each property, linking them to the relevant booking and guest record. This allows you to identify patterns — if multiple guests mention the same issue, it needs addressing — and to respond to reviews promptly and professionally.

The post-departure communication sequence should include a review request, timed to arrive when the guest's experience is still fresh. Making it easy for satisfied guests to leave reviews is one of the most effective things you can do to build your holiday let portfolio's reputation.

## Compliance considerations

Holiday lets may be subject to different regulations than standard tenancies — planning permissions, licensing requirements, tax treatment, and safety standards. Your CRM should track the compliance requirements specific to each holiday let property, with the same automated reminder system used for standard let compliance certificates.

## The landlord relationship

Landlords who invest in holiday lets have different expectations from those with standard lets. They expect higher yields but understand that the management is more intensive. They want regular reporting on occupancy, revenue, and guest feedback.

Your CRM should provide holiday let landlords with clear, regular reports covering bookings, revenue, occupancy rates, maintenance costs, and guest reviews. This transparency justifies the typically higher management fees that holiday let services command.
