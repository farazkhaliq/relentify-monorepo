Goodlord has carved out a specific niche in the UK lettings market: streamlining the lettings transaction. From offer acceptance to move-in, Goodlord digitises referencing, tenancy agreements, rent protection insurance, and deposit registration. It does this well, and for many agents, Goodlord has become an essential part of their lettings workflow.

But Goodlord is not a CRM. It does not manage properties, track pipelines, handle landlord relationships, or provide the broader business management tools that letting agents need. Relentify CRM covers the entire lettings lifecycle — from marketing a property through to ongoing tenancy management — in a single platform.

## Understanding the difference

The first thing to understand is that Goodlord and Relentify CRM are different types of tools, which makes a direct comparison nuanced.

**Goodlord** focuses on the lettings transaction: the period between offer acceptance and move-in. It handles referencing, document signing, insurance products, and compliance checks during this window.

**Relentify CRM** covers the full lettings lifecycle: property marketing, applicant management, viewings, offers, tenancy creation, ongoing management, renewals, and checkout. It also includes CRM features for landlord relationship management and business development.

Some agencies use both types of tool — a CRM for the broader workflow and a transaction platform for the referencing and move-in process. Others prefer a single platform that handles everything.

## Where Goodlord excels

Goodlord's transaction workflow is streamlined and effective. When a letting agent accepts an offer, Goodlord takes over: referencing is initiated, tenancy agreements are generated and signed electronically, insurance products are offered to tenants, and deposit registration is handled.

This workflow reduces the administrative burden during the busiest part of the lettings process. The tenant experience is also improved — everything is handled through a single portal rather than dealing with separate referencing, insurance, and deposit companies.

Goodlord also generates revenue for agents through insurance and service add-ons offered to tenants during the move-in process.

## Where Relentify CRM covers more ground

Relentify CRM handles everything before and after Goodlord's window:

**Before the transaction:**
- Marketing properties across portals
- Matching applicants to suitable properties
- Managing viewings and feedback
- Tracking the pipeline from enquiry to offer

**During the transaction:**
- Relentify eSign for tenancy agreement signing
- Integration with referencing services
- Compliance document tracking

**After the transaction:**
- Ongoing tenancy management
- Rent collection and arrears tracking
- Maintenance request management
- Compliance deadline monitoring
- Renewal management
- Landlord relationship management

## Pricing model

Goodlord typically charges per transaction rather than a monthly subscription. The cost per tenancy setup varies based on the services selected, with referencing, document signing, and insurance each carrying their own fees.

Relentify CRM charges a flat monthly rate of £49 per month with unlimited users and unlimited properties. There are no per-transaction fees.

| | Relentify CRM | Goodlord |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing model | Flat monthly | Per-transaction |
| Monthly cost | £49/month | Varies by volume |
| Per-transaction cost | None | Per-tenancy fees |

For high-volume agencies processing many tenancies per month, Goodlord's per-transaction costs can be significant. However, the revenue from insurance and service add-ons can offset some of this cost.

## Can they work together?

Yes. Some agencies use Relentify CRM as their primary CRM and property management platform, then use Goodlord specifically for the referencing and move-in transaction.

This approach uses each tool for what it does best: Relentify CRM for the broad business management, and Goodlord for the focused transaction workflow. The downside is managing two platforms and the associated cost.

## The single-platform alternative

Relentify CRM covers the transaction process through its own tools: Relentify eSign for tenancy agreement signing, integration with referencing providers, and compliance document management. While this is not as specialised as Goodlord's transaction workflow, it keeps everything on one platform.

For agencies that value simplicity and prefer a single system, Relentify CRM can replace the need for a separate transaction platform.

## Referencing

Goodlord provides in-house referencing that is tightly integrated with its workflow. References are initiated, processed, and returned within the same platform.

Relentify CRM integrates with established referencing providers. The referencing process works through integration rather than being native, which means an extra step compared to Goodlord's seamless experience.

## Insurance and revenue generation

Goodlord offers tenants rent protection insurance and other products during the move-in process. Agents earn commission on these products, which can be meaningful for high-volume agencies.

Relentify CRM does not currently offer embedded insurance products. For agencies where insurance commission is a significant revenue stream, this is a factor in the decision.

## Who should choose which

**Choose Goodlord (alongside a CRM) if** you process a high volume of tenancies and the streamlined transaction workflow saves significant time, you value the embedded insurance and service products that generate commission, and you are willing to run two platforms.

**Choose Relentify CRM (as a single platform) if** you want one platform for your entire lettings workflow from marketing to management, you prefer flat-rate pricing without per-transaction fees, you need CRM features for landlord acquisition and pipeline management, or you want native accounting integration.

**Use both if** you want the best of both worlds and the combined cost is justified by the efficiency gains.

## The bottom line

Goodlord and Relentify CRM serve different but overlapping needs. Goodlord excels at the narrow transaction window between offer and move-in. Relentify CRM covers the entire business lifecycle.

For agencies choosing one platform, Relentify CRM provides more comprehensive coverage. For agencies willing to use specialised tools for specific tasks, Goodlord can complement Relentify CRM's broader functionality. The right choice depends on your volume, your workflow preferences, and how much you value having everything in one place.
