HubSpot has become synonymous with inbound marketing and CRM. Service Hub is their customer service product, built on top of the HubSpot CRM platform. It integrates tightly with Marketing Hub and Sales Hub, creating a unified customer record across the entire journey.

That integration is HubSpot's selling point. But it comes at a cost — both financially and in terms of focus. Service Hub is part of an ecosystem, not a standalone product, which means some helpdesk features feel secondary to HubSpot's core CRM and marketing strengths.

Relentify Connect is a dedicated helpdesk platform, designed from the ground up for customer support. Here is how the two compare.

## Pricing

HubSpot's pricing is tiered and can escalate quickly, especially when you need features from their Professional or Enterprise tiers.

| Plan | Relentify Connect | HubSpot Service Hub |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| Free tier | Yes (2 agents) | Yes (limited) |
| Starter | £12/agent/month | $20/seat/month (~£16) |
| Professional | £25/agent/month | $100/seat/month (~£79) |
| Enterprise | £45/agent/month | $150/seat/month (~£118) |

The jump from HubSpot's Starter to Professional plan is dramatic — from $20 to $100 per seat. This is where most growing teams hit a wall. Features like SLA management, custom reporting, and automation are locked behind the Professional plan, which costs more than three times Relentify Connect's equivalent tier.

HubSpot also requires a minimum number of seats on higher plans and charges an onboarding fee for Professional and Enterprise, which can add thousands to the initial cost.

Relentify Connect includes all of these from the Starter plan at £12/agent/month.

## The all-in-one advantage (and disadvantage)

HubSpot's strength is the unified CRM. When a support agent opens a ticket, they can see the customer's marketing interactions, sales pipeline stage, website activity, and previous support history — all in one place.

This is genuinely valuable for businesses that use HubSpot across marketing, sales, and support. The customer record is comprehensive, and the data flows seamlessly between departments.

The disadvantage is cost. To get the full benefit, you often need Professional or Enterprise plans across multiple Hubs. A business using Marketing Hub Professional, Sales Hub Professional, and Service Hub Professional is looking at several hundred dollars per seat per month.

Relentify Connect provides support-specific customer profiles with conversation history, activity timelines, and integration with Relentify's CRM. For businesses that do not need HubSpot's full marketing and sales automation stack, this provides sufficient customer context at a fraction of the cost.

## Who should choose which?

**Choose Relentify Connect if:**
- You need a dedicated helpdesk with deep support features
- Knowledge base, SLA management, and CSAT at a lower price matter
- Multi-channel support including social and messaging is important
- You want full automation and reporting without paying $100+/seat
- You do not need HubSpot's marketing and sales automation stack

**Choose HubSpot Service Hub if:**
- You already use HubSpot CRM, Marketing Hub, or Sales Hub
- The unified customer record across marketing, sales, and support is critical
- You need deep marketing automation integrated with support
- Your budget accommodates HubSpot's Professional pricing

## The bottom line

HubSpot Service Hub is a good product within the HubSpot ecosystem. If you are already a HubSpot customer, the integration value is real. But as a standalone helpdesk, it is expensive for what it offers — particularly at the Starter level, where core features like knowledge base and SLA management are missing.

Relentify Connect delivers more helpdesk functionality at every tier, at a price that makes sense for businesses that need customer support tools — not a full marketing automation platform.
