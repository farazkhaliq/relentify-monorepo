Kaptur is an established name in UK property inventory software, offering a cloud-based platform for creating inventory, check-in, check-out, and inspection reports. It has been used by independent clerks and letting agencies for some years, building a loyal user base.

Relentify Inventories is a modern alternative that aims to do everything Kaptur does — and more — while keeping pricing straightforward and accessible. If you are evaluating inventory software or considering a switch from Kaptur, here is how the two compare.

## Platform overview

Both Kaptur and Relentify are cloud-based platforms designed for property professionals who need to create structured, photo-rich inventory reports. Both work on mobile devices and sync to a web dashboard for management and report delivery.

| Aspect | Relentify Inventories | Kaptur |
|--------|----------------------|--------|
| Mobile app | iOS and Android | iOS and Android |
| Web dashboard | Full-featured | Yes |
| Offline mode | Yes | Yes |
| Cloud sync | Automatic | Automatic |
| Report types | Check-in, check-out, mid-tenancy, interim | Check-in, check-out, mid-tenancy, inspection |
| Target market | UK residential lettings | UK residential lettings |

Both platforms target the same market. The differences lie in the details of their features, pricing, and the overall user experience.

## Report creation workflow

**Kaptur** uses a structured approach with pre-built templates for different report types. You work through rooms and items in sequence, adding photos and condition notes. The workflow is logical and follows a pattern familiar to experienced clerks.

**Relentify** follows a similar room-by-room structure but puts more emphasis on speed. The interface is designed to minimise navigation — you can capture photos, rate condition, and add notes without switching between screens. Quick-add buttons for common items mean less typing and faster completion.

For clerks who conduct multiple inspections daily, workflow efficiency directly impacts earning potential. If you can complete a report 10 minutes faster, that time compounds over weeks and months.

## Photo and evidence quality

Strong evidence wins deposit disputes. Both platforms understand this, but their approaches to photo handling differ.

| Photo feature | Relentify Inventories | Kaptur |
|---------------|----------------------|--------|
| In-app camera | Yes | Yes |
| Timestamped photos | Yes (automatic) | Yes |
| GPS verification | Yes (automatic) | Limited |
| Photo annotations | Yes (draw, text, arrows) | Basic markup |
| Photo limit | Unlimited | Plan-dependent |
| Resolution | High (preserved) | Standard compression |
| Panoramic capture | No | No |

Relentify includes GPS verification on every photo by default. This metadata proves not just when a photo was taken but that it was taken at the property in question — an additional layer of evidence that strengthens your position in any dispute.

Photo annotations in Relentify are more developed, allowing you to draw attention to specific damage or features with arrows, circles, and text overlays. This makes reports clearer for everyone who reads them, from landlords to adjudicators.

## Condition ratings and consistency

Consistent, objective condition assessments are the foundation of a defensible inventory report.

**Kaptur** uses a condition description system where clerks describe the state of items using text. This gives flexibility but can lead to inconsistency between different clerks or even between the same clerk on different days.

**Relentify** uses a standardised numerical scale with clear definitions for each grade. Every item is rated on the same scale, every time, by every inspector. When a check-in report uses the same scale as a check-out report — even if they were conducted by different people — the comparison is objective and defensible.

## Comparison and check-out reports

This is an area where Relentify has a clear advantage.

When you create a check-out report in Relentify, the platform automatically loads the corresponding check-in data. You see the original photos, condition ratings, and notes alongside your new observations. The system generates a comparison report highlighting every change, making it immediately clear what has deteriorated, what is fair wear and tear, and what might justify a deposit deduction.

Kaptur supports check-out reports that reference the original inventory, but the comparison process requires more manual effort. The clerk needs to cross-reference the two reports, and the final comparison report is less automated.

For agencies processing dozens of check-outs per month, this automation saves hours of administrative time.

## Mobile experience

Both platforms are designed for mobile use, but the experience differs.

**Kaptur's** mobile app is functional and gets the job done. It follows a fairly traditional form-based approach to data capture.

**Relentify's** mobile app is built with a mobile-first philosophy. The interface adapts to phone and tablet screens, with large touch targets, swipe gestures, and a camera workflow optimised for one-handed operation. When you are standing in a property holding your phone in one hand and a set of keys in the other, these design choices make a noticeable difference.

## Who should choose which?

**Choose Relentify if:**
- You want unlimited reports without per-inspection charges
- Automatic comparison reports would save you time
- Team access without per-user pricing matters
- Transparent, published pricing is important to you
- You want stronger photo evidence with GPS verification
- Mobile experience and workflow speed are priorities

**Choose Kaptur if:**
- You are already established on the platform with historical data
- You have a workflow built specifically around Kaptur's template system
- You have a negotiated pricing agreement that works for you
