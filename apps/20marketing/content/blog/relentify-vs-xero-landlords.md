Landlords have specific accounting needs that general-purpose accounting software does not always address well. Tracking rental income across multiple properties, managing tenant deposits, handling maintenance expenses, calculating property-specific profitability, and keeping up with tax obligations all require a system that understands the nuances of property finance.

Xero is one of the most popular accounting platforms in the world, and many landlords use it to manage their finances. But is a generalist tool the best choice when your primary business is property? This comparison examines how Relentify and Xero stack up for landlords specifically.

## Property-Centric vs General Purpose

The fundamental difference between Relentify and Xero lies in their design philosophy. Xero is built to serve businesses of all types, from freelancers and retailers to manufacturers and professional services firms. It does this well, offering a broad feature set that covers standard accounting needs.

Relentify is built as an integrated business platform that includes [accounting](/accounting), [CRM](/crm), [property inventory management](/inventory), and workforce tools. For landlords, this means your accounting, tenant relationships, property records, and inventory reports all live in one place, reducing the need for multiple subscriptions and manual data transfers.

## Tracking Income by Property

Every landlord needs to know how each property is performing. If you own five rental properties, you need to see the income, expenses, and net profit for each one individually.

In Xero, this requires using tracking categories or projects to tag transactions against specific properties. It works, but it is an additional layer of manual categorisation that you or your bookkeeper must maintain consistently. If a transaction is not tagged correctly, your property-level reporting is inaccurate.

Relentify's CRM and accounting integration allows you to link financial transactions to specific properties naturally. Your rental income, maintenance costs, agent fees, insurance, and mortgage interest can all be associated with the correct property, giving you accurate per-property profitability without extra manual effort.

## Tenant Management

Managing tenants is a core part of being a landlord, and it goes beyond accounting. You need to track lease dates, contact information, communication history, maintenance requests, and deposit details.

Xero has no tenant management capabilities. It is an accounting tool. You would need a separate CRM, spreadsheet, or property management system to handle tenant relationships, and then manually reconcile the financial and tenant data.

Relentify's [CRM](/crm) provides a dedicated space for managing tenant and landlord relationships. You can track tenancy dates, store documents, log communications, and link everything to the corresponding financial records. When a tenant's rent payment arrives, you can see it in context alongside their tenancy details and communication history.

## Property Inventories

If you manage furnished or semi-furnished rental properties, inventory reports are essential for protecting deposits and managing property condition. These reports document the state of the property at check-in and check-out, providing evidence in the event of a dispute.

Xero offers nothing in this area. You would need a separate inventory tool and manage it independently.

Relentify includes [property inventory management](/inventory) as part of the platform. You can create detailed inventory reports with photographs, link them to specific properties and tenancies, and reference them alongside your financial records when processing deposit deductions or maintenance charges.

## Expense Management

Landlord expenses are diverse: mortgage interest, insurance premiums, letting agent fees, maintenance and repairs, utility bills during void periods, travel costs for property visits, and professional fees for accountants and solicitors.

Both Xero and Relentify handle expense tracking well. You can categorise expenses, attach receipts, and run reports showing where your money goes. Xero has a slight edge in bank feed integrations, as it connects to a very wide range of banks globally. However, Relentify's bank connection capabilities cover the major banks that most landlords use.

Where Relentify adds value is in linking expenses to specific properties automatically when they are entered through the CRM or inventory modules. This removes the manual tagging step that Xero requires.

## Tax Reporting

Landlords need to report their rental income and expenses for tax purposes. The specific requirements vary by jurisdiction, but the underlying need is the same: accurate figures, properly categorised, with supporting documentation.

Xero provides comprehensive tax reporting features and integrates with many tax filing services. Its reports are well-designed and widely accepted by accountants.

Relentify also provides the standard financial reports that landlords and their accountants need: profit and loss statements, balance sheets, and detailed transaction listings. The per-property breakdown that comes naturally from the integrated system is particularly useful for landlords who need to report on individual properties.

## Scalability

Whether you own two properties or twenty, your accounting system needs to scale without becoming unmanageable.

In Xero, managing a small portfolio is straightforward. As your portfolio grows, the tracking categories and manual tagging become more time-consuming. Larger portfolios may require a more sophisticated setup, potentially involving Xero's project feature or third-party add-ons.

Relentify's integrated approach scales more naturally for property businesses. Adding a new property means creating it in the CRM and linking it to your accounting. All the financial tracking, tenant management, and inventory capabilities follow automatically.

## Pricing Considerations

Xero's pricing is based on the number of invoices and bills you process, with three main tiers. For landlords with a small number of tenants, the lower tiers may be sufficient, but you still need to budget for any additional tools you need for tenant management, inventories, and other property-specific functions.

Relentify's pricing includes accounting, CRM, inventories, and other modules in a single subscription. For landlords who would otherwise need to purchase and manage multiple separate tools, the total cost of ownership is often lower.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Xero has a large ecosystem of third-party apps that extend its functionality. If you need property management features, there are Xero add-ons that provide them, though each adds cost and complexity.

Relentify takes a different approach by building property-relevant features into the core platform. This means fewer integrations to manage, fewer subscriptions to track, and a more cohesive experience. For landlords who value simplicity and efficiency, this integrated approach is often preferable.

## Accountant Access

Many landlords work with external accountants who prepare their tax returns and provide financial advice. Both Xero and Relentify support accountant access, allowing your accountant to view your books, run reports, and make adjustments.

Xero's accountant ecosystem is very well established, and most accountants are familiar with the platform. If your accountant specifically requires Xero, that is a factor worth considering.

Relentify provides accountant access with dedicated tools for accountants managing multiple clients. The per-property reporting that landlords need is available to accountants alongside the standard financial statements they expect.

## Making the Right Choice

For landlords whose property business is their primary focus, an integrated platform that combines accounting with CRM, inventories, and tenant management offers clear advantages. It reduces complexity, eliminates manual data transfers between systems, and provides a joined-up view of your property business.

If your accounting needs extend well beyond property — perhaps you have other businesses with complex requirements — Xero's breadth and flexibility may be the better fit.

For most landlords, the question comes down to whether you want a general tool that you adapt to property, or a platform that understands property from the ground up. Both can do the job. The difference is in how much extra work you have to do to make it happen.
