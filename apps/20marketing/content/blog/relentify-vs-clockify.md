Clockify is one of the most popular free time tracking tools. Its pitch is simple: unlimited users, unlimited tracking, free forever. It has attracted millions of users — freelancers, agencies, and small teams who need to track where their hours go.

But Clockify is a time tracker, not a timesheet management system. If you need GPS-verified clock-ins, shift scheduling, break compliance, and manager approval workflows, Clockify's free tier falls short — and its paid plans may not be the most cost-effective option.

Relentify Timesheets is built for businesses that employ people and need to track their working time accurately, compliantly, and with verification. Here is how the two compare.

## Different tools for different problems

Before comparing features, it is important to understand the fundamental difference:

**Clockify** is a time tracker. It is designed for knowledge workers, freelancers, and project teams who need to log hours against tasks and projects. You start a timer, stop it, and the hours are recorded.

**Relentify Timesheets** is a workforce timesheet system. It is designed for businesses with employees who clock in and out of shifts, work at specific sites, take regulated breaks, and need their hours approved by a manager before payroll.

If you are a freelancer tracking billable hours or a marketing agency tracking project time, Clockify is the right category of tool. If you employ hourly workers, manage shifts, or need to prove when and where employees worked, Relentify Timesheets is purpose-built for that use case.

## GPS and location verification

This is where the tools diverge most sharply.

**Clockify** offers GPS tracking on its Pro plan ($7.99/user/month). It records the location when users start and stop their timer, and tracks routes for field workers. It is useful for knowing where people work, but it is not designed as a verification system.

**Relentify Timesheets** uses GPS as a verification mechanism. Workers clock in at a specific site, the system confirms they are within the geofenced area, and the clock-in is recorded with location data. If they are outside the boundary, the clock-in is flagged. This prevents time fraud — no one can clock in from home when they should be on site.

| Location feature | Relentify Timesheets | Clockify |

| GPS at clock-in | Yes (verified) | Pro plan (tracked) |
| Geofencing | Yes | No |
| Photo at clock-in | Yes | No |
| Location history | Yes | Pro plan |
| Site-based tracking | Yes | No (GPS route) |
| Alerts for violations | Yes | No |

For businesses that need to prove where employees were when they clocked in — construction, cleaning, security, care agencies — Relentify's verification approach is significantly more robust.

## Break tracking and compliance

UK employment law requires specific break entitlements based on shift length. Getting this wrong can result in penalties.

**Clockify** allows users to log breaks manually. There are no automatic break rules, no compliance alerts, and no enforcement of minimum break requirements.

**Relentify Timesheets** automates break compliance:
- Configurable break rules by shift length (e.g., 20 minutes after 6 hours)
- Automatic break deductions when appropriate
- Alerts when breaks are missed or too short
- Break records included in timesheet reports
- Compliance with UK Working Time Regulations

For any business that employs hourly workers, this automation reduces risk and saves management time.

## Shift management

**Relentify Timesheets** is built around shifts:

## Mobile experience

Both platforms have mobile apps. Clockify's app is centred around the timer — start, stop, categorise. Relentify's app is centred around the clock-in experience — arrive at site, verify location, take photo, clock in.

For desk workers tracking project time, Clockify's timer interface is natural. For field workers, site employees, and hourly staff, Relentify's clock-in interface is more appropriate.
