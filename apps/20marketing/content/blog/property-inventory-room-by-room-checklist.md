One of the most common reasons inventories fail in deposit disputes is that they are incomplete. A report that covers the living room in detail but barely mentions the bathroom, or documents the walls but ignores the fixtures, has gaps that undermine its usefulness.

A thorough inventory covers everything. Every room, every surface, every fixture, every item. This room-by-room checklist is designed to make sure nothing gets missed — whether you are a landlord doing your own inventory, a letting agent building a process, or an inventory clerk looking for a comprehensive reference.

## General items for every room

Before diving into room-specific details, these elements should be documented in every room of the property:

- **Ceiling** — type (plastered, artex, exposed beams), colour, condition (cracks, stains, peeling)
- **Walls** — finish (paint, wallpaper, tiles), colour, condition (marks, scuffs, holes, cracks, damp)
- **Floor** — type (carpet, laminate, tile, hardwood, vinyl), colour, condition (stains, wear, damage)
- **Skirting boards** — material, colour, condition (scuffs, chips, gaps)
- **Doors** — material, colour, condition (scratches, dents), handles and locks (working condition)
- **Windows** — type (single/double glazed, UPVC/wood), condition of glass (cracks, scratches), frames, seals, locks, handles
- **Light fittings** — type (ceiling, wall, spotlights), condition, working status
- **Switches and sockets** — condition (cracks, discolouration), working status
- **Radiators** — type, condition (rust, dents), working status
- **Curtains or blinds** — type, colour, condition, working status (do they open and close properly?)

For every observation, take at least one supporting photograph.

## Entrance hall and corridors

The entrance sets the tone for the property and is a high-traffic area that shows wear quickly.

- Front door — exterior and interior condition, locks, handles, letter box, door number
- Doormat or threshold
- Coat hooks or storage
- Hallway carpet or flooring — especially wear patterns near the front door
- Smoke or carbon monoxide detectors — presence and working status
- Alarm system keypads
- Fuse box or consumer unit location
- Staircase — condition of treads, risers, handrail, spindles
- Under-stairs cupboard — contents, condition

## Living room

- Fireplace or feature wall — mantelpiece, surround, hearth, condition
- Built-in shelving or alcoves — condition
- Television aerial point or wall mount — condition
- All furniture (if furnished) — sofas, chairs, tables, TV unit, shelving, lamps
- Soft furnishings — cushions, throws, rugs
- Decorative items — pictures, mirrors, ornaments

## Kitchen

The kitchen is often the most detailed room in an inventory due to the number of appliances and fixtures.

### Fitted kitchen

- Units — type (wall and base), material, colour, condition of doors, handles, hinges, drawers
- Worktops — material, colour, condition (scratches, burns, stains, chips)
- Splashback or tiling — condition, grout colour and condition
- Sink — type (single/double, material), condition, taps (working, dripping?), plugs
- Draining board — condition

### Appliances

For each appliance, note the make, model (if visible), colour, and condition:

- Oven — exterior and interior condition, shelves, trays, glass door
- Hob — type (gas, electric, induction), condition of rings or plates, control knobs
- Extractor fan — working status, condition of filters
- Fridge — exterior and interior, shelves, drawers, seals, temperature
- Freezer — exterior and interior, drawers, ice build-up
- Washing machine — exterior, drum interior, detergent drawer, seals, working status
- Tumble dryer — exterior, drum, lint filter, working status
- Dishwasher — exterior, interior, racks, seals, working status
- Microwave — exterior, interior, turntable, working status
- Boiler — location, make, model, condition, pressure reading

### Kitchen contents (if furnished)

- Crockery — plates, bowls, cups, mugs (quantity and condition)
- Cutlery — knives, forks, spoons (quantity and condition)
- Cooking utensils — spatulas, wooden spoons, tin opener, etc.
- Pots and pans — type, quantity, condition
- Glasses — drinking glasses, wine glasses (quantity and condition)
- Miscellaneous — chopping boards, dish rack, kettle, toaster, bin

## Bedrooms

For each bedroom:

- Bed frame — type (single, double, king), material, condition
- Mattress — type, condition (stains, sagging, firmness)
- Bedding (if provided) — duvet, pillows, covers, condition
- Wardrobe — type (freestanding or built-in), material, condition of doors, shelves, rail, handles
- Drawers — type, material, condition, smooth operation
- Bedside tables — condition
- Dressing table and mirror — condition
- Lamps — working status, condition

## Bathroom

- Bath — type (standard, roll-top, corner), material, colour, condition (chips, stains, scratches)
- Shower — type (over bath, separate cubicle, wet room), condition of screen or curtain, head, hose, controls
- Toilet — condition (bowl, seat, lid, cistern), flush mechanism working
- Sink or basin — type, condition, taps (working, dripping?), plugs
- Vanity unit or cupboard — condition
- Tiles — condition (cracks, chips, missing grout, discolouration)
- Grouting and sealant — condition (mould, gaps, peeling)
- Mirror or cabinet — condition
- Towel rail or radiator — working status, condition
- Extractor fan — working status
- Shaving point — working status

## Separate toilet

If the property has a separate WC:

- Toilet — condition, flush mechanism
- Sink — condition, taps
- Flooring — condition
- Ventilation — window or fan

## Utility room

- Washing machine and dryer (if not in kitchen)
- Utility sink or slop sink
- Shelving or storage
- Boiler (if located here)
- Water tank or cylinder

## Loft or attic

If accessible:

- Access hatch — condition, does it open and close properly?
- Boarding — present? condition?
- Insulation — type, condition
- Water tanks — condition
- Stored items — anything left by the landlord

## Garage

- Door — type (up-and-over, roller, side-hinged), condition, locks, working mechanism
- Floor — condition (oil stains, cracks)
- Electrics — lights, sockets, working status
- Shelving or storage
- Contents — any items left by the landlord

## Garden and external areas

### Front garden

- Lawn — condition (mowed, overgrown, bare patches)
- Borders and beds — condition, planting
- Path or driveway — material, condition (cracks, weeds, stains)
- Fencing or walls — type, condition (leaning, broken panels, gaps)
- Gate — condition, working status

### Rear garden

- Lawn — condition
- Patio or decking — material, condition (slippery, stained, damaged)
- Borders and beds — condition
- Fencing or walls — condition
- Shed — condition (roof, door, lock, floor, windows)
- Greenhouse — condition
- Garden furniture (if provided) — type, condition
- Bins — type, quantity, condition
- Washing line or rotary dryer — condition
- Outdoor lighting — working status
- Outside tap — working status

## Meters and utilities

- Gas meter — location, reading, photograph
- Electricity meter — location, reading, photograph
- Water meter — location, reading, photograph (if applicable)
- Stopcock location
- Gas shut-off valve location

## Keys

- Number of sets
- Description of each key (front door, back door, window locks, garage, shed, meter cupboard, alarm, mailbox)
- Fobs, codes, or electronic access devices
- Photograph of all keys laid out together

## Safety compliance

- Smoke alarms — location, working status, battery or mains powered
- Carbon monoxide detector — location, working status
- Fire extinguisher or blanket — location, condition (if present)
- Gas safety certificate — current? (note, do not verify — just note whether displayed)
- Electrical safety certificate — current? (note whether displayed)

## Making the checklist work for you

This list is comprehensive, and not every item will apply to every property. A studio flat has no garden section. An unfurnished property has no furniture to list. Adapt the checklist to match the property, but use it as a starting point to ensure you have considered everything.
