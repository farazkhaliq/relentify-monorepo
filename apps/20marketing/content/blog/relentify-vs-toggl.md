But Toggl Track is a time tracker, not a timesheet management system. The distinction matters. If you manage a workforce of hourly employees, need verified clock-ins, and must comply with working time regulations, Toggl's elegant timer may not be the right tool for the job.

Relentify Timesheets is built for workforce time management. Here is how the two compare.

## The timer vs the clock

Toggl Track's one-click timer is its defining feature. Click to start, click to stop, add a description and project. It is elegant and frictionless for desk workers tracking their day.

Relentify Timesheets uses a clock-in/clock-out model. Workers arrive at a site, open the app, verify their location, optionally take a photo, and clock in. At the end of their shift, they clock out. The system records the exact hours with proof of location.

Neither approach is wrong — they serve different workforces. But if you manage employees who work on site and need verified records, the timer model does not provide the accountability that the clock-in model does.

## GPS and verification

Toggl Track does not include GPS verification. It is a trust-based system — users log their time, and the data is taken at face value.

Relentify Timesheets verifies time records with:
- GPS location at clock-in and clock-out
- Geofencing around work sites
- Optional photo verification
- Continuous GPS pings during shifts (configurable)
- Flagging of anomalies (e.g., clocking in from an unexpected location)

For industries where accurate, verifiable time records are essential — construction, cleaning, security, care, hospitality — this verification is not optional, it is a requirement.
