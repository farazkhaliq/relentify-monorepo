Happy Inspector is a property inspection platform that has gained traction with property managers and letting agents who want to digitise their inspection process. It offers customisable templates, photo capture, and cloud-based report management.

Relentify Inventories is built with a similar goal — replacing paper-based and spreadsheet inventories with a modern, mobile-first platform — but with a sharper focus on UK residential lettings and a pricing model that favours high-volume users.

Here is how the two platforms compare across the features and factors that matter most to property professionals.

## Core approach

**Happy Inspector** positions itself as a general-purpose property inspection tool. It is used across residential, commercial, and strata management, with customisable templates that can be adapted to different inspection types. The platform is popular in Australia and has a growing presence in other markets.

**Relentify Inventories** is purpose-built for UK residential lettings. Every feature — from condition rating scales to comparison reports to deposit dispute support — is designed around the specific needs of letting agents, landlords, and inventory clerks operating within the UK tenancy framework.

This difference in focus affects everything from the default templates to the compliance features available out of the box.

This is not something most general-purpose inspection tools offer.

## Workflow and speed

Time spent on each inspection directly affects profitability, particularly for independent clerks who charge per report.

**Happy Inspector** guides users through inspections using customisable checklists. The interface is clean and functional, though some users report that the customisation options — while powerful — add complexity to the initial setup.

**Relentify** prioritises speed of use. The interface is designed to minimise taps: capture a photo, add a condition rating, add a note if needed, move on. Room templates pre-populate common items, but you can add or remove items on the fly without navigating away from the current section.

For clerks conducting multiple inspections per day, these small efficiency gains compound. An extra five minutes saved per inspection across four inspections per day is an extra 80+ hours per year — time that translates directly into either additional revenue or earlier finishes.

## Integration

Happy Inspector offers integrations with property management platforms common in the Australian market, including PropertyMe and Console Cloud. For UK-based users, these integrations may be less relevant.

Relentify focuses on integrations common in the UK market and provides API access for custom integrations with lettings software and property management platforms.

## Who should choose which?

**Choose Relentify if:**
- You operate in UK residential lettings
- You want unlimited reports and team access without per-user charges
- Automatic comparison reports matter for your deposit disputes
