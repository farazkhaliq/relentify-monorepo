When it comes to property inventory management, letting agents and landlords face a fundamental choice: handle inventories in-house with software, or outsource the entire process to a third-party service. Relentify Inventories and No Letting Go represent these two very different approaches.

No Letting Go is the UK's largest outsourced inventory provider, with a franchise network of clerks who physically attend properties and produce reports on your behalf. Relentify Inventories is a modern software platform that gives you (or your team) the tools to create professional, photo-rich inventory reports yourself.

Both have their place. But the right choice depends on your volume, budget, and how much control you want over the process.

## How the two models work

**No Letting Go** operates as a managed service. You book an appointment, one of their clerks visits the property, conducts the inspection, and delivers a completed report — typically within 48 hours. You pay per report, with prices varying by property size and location.

**Relentify Inventories** is a self-service platform. You (or a member of your team) visit the property with a phone or tablet, use the app to photograph every room and item, add condition notes, and generate a branded PDF report on the spot. You pay a monthly subscription regardless of how many reports you produce.

## Control and flexibility

Outsourcing means handing over quality control. No Letting Go employs trained clerks and has quality assurance processes, but you are still relying on someone else to represent your standards. If a clerk misses a detail or uses inconsistent language, you may not discover the issue until a dispute arises months later.

With Relentify, your own staff conduct the inspections. They know the properties, they know the landlords' expectations, and they can apply your agency's standards consistently. The software guides them through a structured process — room by room, item by item — so nothing gets missed, but the judgement calls remain with people who understand the context.

There is also the question of scheduling. With an outsourced service, you need to book appointments, coordinate availability, and work around the clerk's diary. With your own team and Relentify, you can conduct an inventory whenever it suits your schedule — including at short notice when a tenancy ends unexpectedly.

## Branding and client relationships

With No Letting Go, the report carries their branding, not yours. The clerk wears their uniform. The landlord sees a third-party company handling what many consider a core part of the letting service.

With Relentify, every report carries your agency's logo and branding. To the landlord, the inventory is part of the service you provide — reinforcing the value of your management fee rather than highlighting that you have outsourced a key responsibility.

For agents competing for new instructions, demonstrating that you handle inventories professionally and in-house can be a differentiator.

## When outsourcing makes sense

No Letting Go and similar services are a reasonable choice in specific circumstances:

- **Solo landlords** with one or two properties who do not want to learn software or conduct inspections themselves
- **Agents with no available staff** during peak periods who need overflow capacity
- **Dispute-heavy portfolios** where having an independent third party conduct the inspection adds perceived neutrality

If you fall into one of these categories, an outsourced service can save you time — though it will cost you considerably more per property.

## When software makes sense

Relentify Inventories is the better choice when:

- **You manage more than a handful of properties** and the per-report costs would add up
- **You want control** over quality, branding, and scheduling
- **Speed matters** — you need reports available immediately, not in 48 hours
- **You want to reduce costs** without reducing report quality
- **Consistency is important** — your team applies the same standards across every property

For most letting agents, this is the more practical and cost-effective approach. The initial investment in learning the software is minimal — Relentify is designed to be picked up in minutes, not days — and the ongoing savings are substantial.

## The hybrid approach

Some agents use a combination: software for routine check-ins and mid-tenancy inspections, with an outsourced service for check-outs where disputes are more likely. This can work, but it introduces inconsistency between reports produced by different systems, which can weaken your position if a case goes to the TDS or DPS.

A more robust approach is to use Relentify for everything, ensuring that check-in and check-out reports use the same format, the same condition scale, and the same room structure — making like-for-like comparisons straightforward in any dispute.

## Data and history

With Relentify, all your inventory data lives in one place. You can pull up the full history of a property across multiple tenancies, compare condition at check-in versus check-out, and track recurring issues. This is your data, stored securely, accessible whenever you need it.

With an outsourced service, your reports are typically delivered as PDFs. Searching across them, comparing across tenancies, or extracting trends requires manual effort.
