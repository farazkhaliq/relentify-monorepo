Zoho Books is the accounting arm of Zoho's massive software ecosystem. With over 50 products spanning CRM, project management, HR, and more, Zoho positions itself as the everything-suite for businesses. Zoho Books benefits from this ecosystem through integrations with other Zoho products, but it also carries the burden of being one piece in an enormous puzzle.

Relentify takes the opposite approach: build accounting software that does not require you to buy into an entire ecosystem to get the most out of it.

## Pricing comparison

Zoho Books offers a free plan for businesses with under £37,500 in annual revenue (a threshold that limits its usefulness), plus three paid tiers: Standard, Professional, and Premium.

| Feature | Relentify (Growth) | Zoho Books (Professional) |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price | £29/month | £29/month |
| Users | Unlimited | 5 |
| Invoices | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Purchase orders | Included | Professional only |
| Multi-currency | Included | Standard and above |
| Expense claims | Included | Standard and above |
| Approval workflows | Included | Professional only |
| Project tracking | Included | Professional only |
| Multi-entity | Included | Not available |
| Additional user cost | Free | £3-5/user/month |

At the same price point, Relentify includes unlimited users while Zoho Books limits you to five on the Professional plan. Additional users cost £3 to £5 each per month depending on the tier. For a team of fifteen people, that is an extra £30 to £50 per month with Zoho Books.

## The ecosystem advantage and disadvantage

Zoho's strength is its ecosystem. If you use Zoho CRM, Zoho Projects, Zoho People, and Zoho Books together, data flows between applications reasonably well. For businesses already committed to the Zoho ecosystem, this integration has real value.

The disadvantage is that Zoho Books is optimised as part of this ecosystem rather than as a standalone product. Some features feel like they are designed to push you toward other Zoho products rather than solving problems within Books itself. Reporting integrations, for example, work best with Zoho Analytics — a separate product with its own pricing.

Relentify is designed to be complete on its own. Reporting, expense management, project tracking, and multi-entity management are all built in rather than distributed across separate products.

## Invoicing

Both platforms handle invoicing competently. Zoho Books supports custom templates, recurring invoices, payment reminders, and online payment acceptance.

Relentify matches these features and adds streamlined quote-to-invoice conversion, credit note management integrated directly into the invoicing workflow, and partial payment recording from the invoice screen. The overall invoicing workflow involves fewer clicks and feels more responsive.

## Expense management

Zoho Books includes expense tracking and receipt capture on its Standard plan and above. Approval workflows are only available on the Professional plan.

Relentify includes expense claims with approval workflows, mileage tracking, and automated categorisation on every paid plan. There is no need to upgrade to a higher tier to get expense approvals.

## Reporting

Zoho Books provides a reasonable set of reports: profit and loss, balance sheet, cash flow, tax reports, and project reports on higher plans. Custom reports are available but require navigating through a somewhat complex report builder.

For more advanced reporting, Zoho pushes users toward Zoho Analytics, which is a separate product with its own subscription.

Relentify includes all reporting within the platform itself. Cash flow forecasting, financial health scores, consolidated reporting, and custom reports are all accessible without needing a separate analytics product.

## Multi-entity management

Zoho Books does not support managing multiple entities from a single account. Each entity requires its own Zoho Books organisation, and consolidated reporting across entities requires Zoho Analytics.

Relentify supports multiple entities on a single subscription with instant switching, consolidated reports, and intercompany transaction management.

## Purchase orders

Zoho Books includes purchase orders on its Professional plan. The purchase order workflow is functional, with the ability to create, send, and convert to bills.

Relentify includes purchase orders on every paid plan with approval workflows, escalation rules for overdue approvals, and automatic conversion to bills on receipt.

## Bank reconciliation

Both platforms offer automatic bank feeds and transaction matching. Zoho Books' reconciliation interface is clean and functional, with categorisation rules for recurring transactions.

Relentify's reconciliation works similarly, with the addition of a learning rule engine that improves match suggestions over time based on your categorisation patterns.

## VAT compliance

Both platforms support MTD for VAT and can submit returns directly to HMRC. Zoho Books handles standard VAT schemes well.

Relentify adds support for the flat rate scheme, reverse charge VAT, and period locking to prevent changes to submitted periods.

## Project tracking

Zoho Books includes project tracking on its Professional plan, with time tracking, expense allocation, and basic project profitability reports. This feature is well-integrated but only available on higher tiers.

Relentify includes project tracking on every paid plan with profitability reporting, time and expense allocation, and the ability to invoice directly from tracked project time.

## User interface

Zoho Books' interface is clean and functional, though it carries some of the design conventions shared across the Zoho ecosystem. Navigation can feel overwhelming because of the breadth of features, and some settings are nested deeply within menus.

Relentify's interface is designed for accounting specifically, not as a template shared across dozens of products. Navigation is intuitive, common tasks are accessible quickly, and the design is consistent throughout.

## Automation

Zoho Books includes workflow automation rules that can trigger actions based on events — sending reminders, updating fields, or notifying users. This is a useful feature, though configuring automations requires some technical comfort.

Relentify includes automation features like automatic payment reminders, recurring invoice generation, and expense categorisation rules. These are configured through simpler interfaces that do not require workflow-builder knowledge.

## Support

Zoho Books offers email support on all plans and phone support on higher tiers. Response times can vary, and the support team covers the entire Zoho product range rather than specialising in Books.

Relentify provides live chat support on every plan with AI-assisted responses for common questions and human agents for complex issues.

## Who should choose which

**Choose Zoho Books if** you are already heavily invested in the Zoho ecosystem (Zoho CRM, Zoho People, Zoho Projects) and the cross-product integration justifies the complexity.

**Choose Relentify if** you want a complete accounting platform without needing to subscribe to multiple products, you need unlimited users without per-seat charges, or you want multi-entity management, purchase orders, and expense approval workflows included on every plan.

## The bottom line

Zoho Books is a capable accounting product within a vast ecosystem. If you are already a Zoho customer, the integration between products has genuine value. But if you are evaluating accounting software on its own merits, Relentify offers more features at the same price point, with unlimited users, built-in multi-entity management, and comprehensive reporting that does not require a separate analytics subscription.

The best accounting software is the one that gives you what you need without requiring you to buy into an entire product universe. Relentify delivers that — a complete accounting platform that stands on its own.
