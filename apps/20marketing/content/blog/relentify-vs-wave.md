Wave's pitch is simple and powerful: free accounting software. For sole traders and micro-businesses just getting started, the idea of not paying for accounting software is understandably attractive. Wave does deliver on that promise — its core accounting, invoicing, and receipt scanning are genuinely free.

But free comes with trade-offs. Wave's feature set is limited, its support is minimal on the free tier, and it lacks the tools that growing businesses need. The question is not whether Wave is good for free software — it is — but whether free software is good enough for your business.

## What Wave offers for free

Wave's free tier includes double-entry accounting, unlimited invoicing, receipt scanning, and bank connections. For a sole trader sending a few invoices a month and tracking basic expenses, this covers the essentials.

Wave makes money through its payment processing (2.9% plus 60p per transaction) and its payroll add-on. The core accounting remains free, subsidised by these paid services.

Even on the free tier, Relentify includes expense claims, mileage tracking, quotes, credit notes, and MTD-compliant VAT returns — features that Wave either does not offer or charges for.

## Invoicing comparison

Wave's invoicing is functional but basic. You can create and send invoices, set up recurring invoices, and accept online payments (with Wave's transaction fees). Templates are limited, and customisation options are minimal.

Relentify's free tier includes more invoicing features: quote-to-invoice conversion, credit note management, automated payment reminders, and fully customisable templates. You can record partial payments directly from the invoice screen and attach documents to invoices.

## Reporting

This is where the gap between free and comprehensive becomes clear. Wave offers profit and loss, balance sheet, sales tax, and aged receivables reports. That covers the minimum, but it leaves out reports that accountants and business owners regularly need.

| Report | Relentify (Free) | Wave |
|---|---|---|
| Profit & Loss | Yes | Yes |
| Balance Sheet | Yes | Yes |
| Cash Flow Statement | Yes | No |
| Trial Balance | Yes | No |
| Aged Receivables | Yes | Yes |
| Aged Payables | Yes | No |
| General Ledger | Yes | No |

Wave does not offer a cash flow statement, trial balance, aged payables report, or general ledger. If your accountant needs any of these for year-end preparation, you will need to export data and build them manually.

Relentify's free tier includes all standard financial reports. Upgrading to a paid plan adds cash flow forecasting, financial health scores, and custom reports.

## VAT and tax compliance

Wave offers basic sales tax tracking but its MTD support for UK businesses is limited. If you need to submit VAT returns directly to HMRC, Wave does not provide a seamless experience.

Relentify supports Making Tax Digital on every plan, including the free tier. You can calculate your VAT return, review the nine-box breakdown, and submit directly to HMRC.

## Expense management

Wave does not include expense claims or mileage tracking. You can categorise transactions from your bank feed, but there is no workflow for team members to submit expense claims with receipts for approval.

Relentify includes expense claims and mileage tracking on the free tier. On paid plans, you also get approval workflows and automated categorisation rules.

## Bank reconciliation

Both platforms offer automatic bank feeds and transaction matching. Wave's reconciliation interface is straightforward and works well for simple matching.

Relentify's reconciliation offers similar ease of use with the addition of rule-based categorisation that improves over time. The interface is faster for high-volume reconciliation, which matters more as your transaction count grows.

## Scalability

This is the fundamental question when evaluating Wave. It works well for its target market — sole traders and very small businesses — but it does not scale.

Wave does not offer purchase orders, multi-entity management, project tracking, or advanced reporting. There is no approval workflow for expenses or bills. When your business grows to the point where you need these features, you face a migration to a different platform.

Relentify scales from a free sole trader plan to a growth plan that includes purchase orders, multi-entity management, project tracking, and consolidated reporting. You can start free and grow without needing to switch platforms.

## Support

Wave's free tier includes community forum support and email support with longer response times. There is no phone support or live chat on the free plan.

Relentify provides live chat support on every plan, including the free tier. AI-assisted responses handle common questions immediately, and human agents are available for complex issues.

## Payment processing

Wave offers integrated payment processing at 2.9% plus 60p per credit card transaction and 1% for bank payments. This is how Wave subsidises its free accounting product.

Relentify integrates with Stripe, which means you can use your existing merchant rates. For businesses processing significant payment volume, the difference in processing fees can be substantial.

## Data security and privacy

Wave is owned by H&R Block and stores data on servers in North America. For UK businesses, this means your financial data is held outside the UK and EU.

Relentify stores data on UK and EU servers, which may be important for businesses with data residency requirements or clients who expect their financial information to remain within their jurisdiction.

## Who should choose which

**Choose Wave if** you are a sole trader with minimal accounting needs, you do not need MTD-compliant VAT returns, you are comfortable with basic reporting, and you genuinely cannot afford any accounting software cost.

**Choose Relentify (Free)** if you want a free tier that includes more features than Wave, you need MTD-compliant VAT returns, you want expense claims and mileage tracking, or you anticipate growing and want to avoid migrating platforms later.

**Choose Relentify (Paid)** if you need purchase orders, multi-entity management, project tracking, advanced reporting, or any features beyond basic accounting.

## The real cost of free

Wave is genuinely free for its core accounting product, and for some businesses that is the right choice. But "free" has hidden costs: limited reporting means more time with spreadsheets, no expense management means manual tracking, and no MTD support means potential compliance issues.

Relentify's free tier offers more features than Wave while matching the price: zero. And when you are ready to grow, upgrading is a matter of choosing a plan rather than migrating to a new platform.

The cheapest software is not always the one with the lowest sticker price. It is the one that saves you the most time and prevents the most problems. For most UK small businesses, Relentify's free tier does both better than Wave.
