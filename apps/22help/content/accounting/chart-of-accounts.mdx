## What this does

Displays every account in your chart of accounts, grouped by type. Each account has a code, name, and type that determines where it appears in your reports. Relentify sets up a standard chart when you create your organisation, and you can add your own accounts as needed.

## When to use it

Review your chart of accounts when setting up your business, creating journal entries, or checking which accounts are available for categorising transactions.

## Step by step

1. Go to **Chart of Accounts** from the main navigation
2. You will see all accounts listed with their code, name, and type
3. Use the search bar to find a specific account by name or code
4. Filter by account type to narrow the list
5. Note the code ranges that determine account type:
   - **1000-1999** — Assets (bank accounts, trade debtors, stock)
   - **2000-2999** — Liabilities (trade creditors, VAT, loans)
   - **3000-3999** — Equity (share capital, retained earnings)
   - **4000-4999** — Income (sales, other revenue)
   - **5000-6999** — Cost of Goods Sold (materials, direct labour)
   - **7000-9998** — Expenses (rent, utilities, wages, marketing)
6. Click any account row to see its transaction history
7. To add a new account, click **Create Account** and assign a code within the correct range
8. Inactive accounts are greyed out but still visible — toggle the filter to hide them
